# Institutions and Character Catalog Framework

Status: draft foundation

## Purpose

Define the organizations and recurring people through which Econovaria communicates policy, business, market, contract, and story content.

Institutions create durable interests. Characters make those interests understandable. Neither should be used as a decorative narrator disconnected from authority or gameplay.

## Institution categories

- national government office;
- central bank or monetary authority;
- regulator;
- development agency;
- commercial association;
- public company or state enterprise;
- university or research consortium;
- labor organization;
- environmental or civic organization;
- news organization;
- international forum;
- financial institution;
- insurer;
- security or infrastructure authority.

## Institution record requirements

Every institution should define:

- stable institution ID;
- official name;
- short name;
- country or global scope;
- institution category;
- legal or political status;
- mandate;
- public objectives;
- operational powers;
- limits of authority;
- economic priorities;
- narrative priorities;
- industries and indicators monitored;
- countries and institutions it cooperates with;
- conflicts and rivalries;
- contracts it may issue;
- events it may trigger or respond to;
- interactions it may send;
- rewards or approvals it may authorize;
- public trust baseline;
- visual identity and asset needs;
- maturity and review status.

## Character record requirements

Every recurring character should define:

- stable character ID;
- full name;
- country;
- institution;
- role and authority;
- public biography;
- relevant expertise;
- economic priorities;
- institutional incentives;
- personal risk tolerance;
- communication style;
- preferred vocabulary;
- information they can access;
- information they cannot credibly know;
- allies;
- rivals;
- conflicts of interest;
- story arcs;
- contract and interaction associations;
- decision reactions;
- status changes;
- portrait and accessibility requirements.

## Character design rules

- A character represents a credible perspective, not a morality label.
- A character may be self-interested without being dishonest.
- A character's authority must match the requested action.
- A character should not approve rewards, policy, or contracts outside their institutional role.
- No character should know hidden player information without an explicit system basis.
- Characters should remember major player decisions when the runtime system supports it.
- Character copy should remain understandable without relying on stereotypes or parody accents.
- Country identity should not be represented by one character alone.

## Opening global institutions

### Starfall Meridian Forum

ID: `institution.global.starfall-meridian-forum.v1`

Country base: Lumenor

Category: international forum

Mandate:

- organize Meridian Corridor negotiations;
- publish shared proposals;
- coordinate committees;
- record nonbinding and binding agreements;
- provide neutral meeting infrastructure.

Limits:

- cannot independently finance or construct the Corridor;
- cannot compel national compliance without ratified terms;
- relies on participating governments for enforcement.

Primary tensions:

- legitimacy versus speed;
- transparency versus confidential negotiation;
- equal representation versus contribution-based influence.

## Required roster expansion

The initial production pack should include at least:

- two recurring perspectives per country;
- one business or company perspective per major company arc;
- labor representation;
- community or consumer representation;
- independent journalism;
- academic or analytical perspective;
- environmental perspective where relevant;
- financial and regulatory perspectives.

No country file is complete if all of its recurring voices come from government or elite institutions.

## Character-response matrix

For each major decision, document:

- supportive response;
- critical response;
- conditional response;
- no-authority response;
- response after prior trust gain;
- response after prior trust loss;
- response when the decision is unavailable or expired.

## Review requirements

- naming review;
- country and cultural sensitivity review;
- authority review;
- voice consistency review;
- conflict-of-interest review;
- duplicate-role review;
- interaction coverage review;
- portrait accessibility and representation review.
