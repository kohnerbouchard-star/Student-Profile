## Player promise

Dravenlok offers stable industrial work, technical advancement, manufacturing entrepreneurship, and a chance to build wealth through the physical systems that support the entire continent.

The player arrives because Meridian expansion has increased demand for steel, rail equipment, machinery, construction vehicles, maintenance, and industrial coordination.

Dravenlok rewards reliability and production. It also struggles with rigid institutions, pollution, import dependence, and pressure to place national capacity above individual choice.

## Arrival scene

The player arrives at Ironhold Central Works Station during a major industrial recruitment campaign.

Visible signals:

- rail equipment leaving enormous factories;
- technical schools advertising paid training;
- worker housing surrounding industrial districts;
- older neighborhoods affected by pollution and redevelopment;
- public campaigns promising national renewal through Meridian contracts.

Immediate need:

Decide whether to accept employer housing tied to a factory contract or preserve job mobility through more expensive independent housing.

## Initial economic paths

### Industrial employment

Work in production support, quality assurance, maintenance, scheduling, procurement, or technical administration.

Strengths:

- reliable income;
- paid training;
- clear advancement;
- direct Meridian relevance.

Risks:

- employer dependence;
- demanding schedules;
- workplace safety pressure;
- wartime production obligations.

### Technical apprenticeship

Enter machinery, rail, welding, electrical, maintenance, or industrial-operations training.

Strengths:

- accessible skill growth;
- durable occupation;
- strong community identity.

Risks:

- lower early income;
- service commitments;
- limited flexibility before qualification.

### Supplier and workshop business

Provide parts, repairs, tools, training, transport support, workwear, or small-batch manufacturing.

Strengths:

- scalable local demand;
- practical entrepreneurship;
- multiple industrial clients.

Risks:

- capital and equipment requirements;
- late payments;
- dependence on large manufacturers;
- energy-cost exposure.

### Industrial and market analysis

Study production capacity, input costs, company demand, labor conditions, and procurement.

Strengths:

- institutional access;
- market relevance;
- influence over major investment.

Risks:

- political pressure;
- incomplete production data;
- conflict between national output and worker welfare.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Orsa Bren

Role: industrial-settlement program supervisor

Orsa helped place the player in temporary housing and training. She believes industrial immigration can renew Ironhold if workers are allowed to become permanent members of society.

Expectation:

The player should complete training and maintain a reliable work record.

### Local friend: Tarek Junn

Role: rail-equipment apprentice

Tarek is proud of Dravenlok’s manufacturing tradition but frustrated by outdated safety practices and limited advancement for younger workers.

Goal:

Open a small precision workshop with modern equipment.

### Rival peer: Vesna Raal

Role: immigrant production planner

Vesna believes management access is the fastest route out of worker dependency. She prioritizes output, deadlines, and institutional favor.

Relationship range:

competitor, manager, business partner, or person whose decisions endanger trust.

### Gatekeeper: Ilya Dren

Existing character: `character.dravenlok.ilya-dren.v1`

Ilya represents workers and industrial accountability. He can connect the player to safer training and collective support, but he expects the player to challenge reckless production demands.

## Fracture phase

Other countries question Dravenlok’s procurement guarantees while demand for industrial capacity increases.

Player consequences:

- overtime and wages may rise;
- imported energy and components become more expensive;
- pressure grows to meet unrealistic schedules;
- small suppliers struggle with financing;
- Tarek sees unsafe shortcuts;
- Vesna advances by promising higher output.

## Attack and war transformation

After the Meridian attack, Dravenlok’s industrial system shifts toward emergency production and infrastructure repair.

Economic changes:

- machinery and steel demand rise;
- civilian products become less available;
- energy and food imports face pressure;
- strategic procurement expands;
- workplace capacity becomes politically sensitive.

Personal changes:

- the player’s employer may receive mandatory priorities;
- Orsa faces pressure to bind workers more tightly to strategic firms;
- Tarek may be injured or reassigned if safety deteriorates;
- Vesna may gain authority over emergency production.

Political changes:

- labor dissent may be framed as a threat to national output;
- foreign workers face loyalty scrutiny;
- state and company authority become harder to separate.

The offer may require remaining with an employer that provides stability but ignores safety, worker voice, or environmental harm.

The player must weigh:

- public need;
