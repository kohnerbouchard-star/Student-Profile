## Player promise

Solvend offers advanced careers, research prestige, technology wealth, and rapid upward mobility for people with scarce skills or the ability to learn quickly.

The player arrives because Meridian contractors need engineers, analysts, technicians, operators, educators, and specialized support workers.

The opportunity is real. So are credential barriers, living costs, intellectual-property controls, and competition for status.

## Arrival scene

The player arrives through Aurora Spire’s integrated transit and professional-intake complex.

Visible signals:

- research campuses and precision factories;
- employers advertising accelerated training;
- high-cost housing clustered around technical districts;
- public warnings about talent shortages;
- security notices concerning strategic technology and data access.

Immediate need:

Verify whether the player’s foreign credential qualifies for direct employment, provisional practice, or retraining.

## Initial economic paths

### Advanced-systems employment

Work in engineering support, testing, data operations, maintenance, or technical administration.

Strengths:

- high income potential;
- strong advancement;
- direct Meridian relevance.

Risks:

- credential dependence;
- confidentiality restrictions;
- demanding performance standards;
- wartime strategic control.

### Accelerated technical training

Enter a paid or subsidized program tied to labor shortages.

Strengths:

- accessible skill growth;
- structured career path;
- relationship-building opportunities.

Risks:

- temporary low income;
- training debt or service obligation;
- uncertain recognition outside the program.

### Technology services business

Provide testing, documentation, translation, maintenance, workflow, design support, or specialized education.

Strengths:

- scalable demand;
- lower capital than manufacturing;
- broad client access.

Risks:

- client concentration;
- licensing and data rules;
- delayed payment;
- intellectual-property disputes.

### Research and market analysis

Study advanced-industry companies, supply constraints, talent flows, and Meridian demand.

Strengths:

- access to high-value information;
- connection to institutions and markets;
- flexible professional identity.

Risks:

- speculative volatility;
- uncertain forecasts;
- suspicion when foreign relationships overlap strategic research.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Iven Sar

Role: technical-talent intake officer

Iven helped the player enter Solvend through a shortage-occupation route. He believes the country needs foreign talent but worries institutions treat people as temporary inputs rather than future citizens.

Expectation:

The player should complete required training and professional reporting.

### Local friend: Liora Fen

Role: systems-testing technician

Liora is capable, overworked, and trying to move from contract work into a permanent research team.

Conflict:

She supports immigration but fears employers use talent shortages to normalize unstable employment for everyone.

### Rival peer: Tevan Aris

Role: immigrant research associate

Tevan is brilliant, ambitious, and willing to attach himself to powerful institutions for access and status.

Relationship range:

collaborator, professional rival, source of opportunity, or person who claims credit when trust breaks.

### Gatekeeper: Amara Tey

Existing character: `character.solvend.amara-tey.v1`

## Fracture phase

Technology ownership and worker mobility become political issues.

Player consequences:

- wages rise in selected skills;
- confidentiality restrictions expand;
- foreign collaboration becomes harder;
- housing costs increase around strategic campuses;
- Liora is offered more work without greater security;
- Tevan gains access by accepting tighter institutional control.

## Attack and war transformation

After the Meridian attack, Solvend’s technology sector becomes central to communications, logistics, verification, and defense resilience.

Economic changes:

- strategic technology demand rises;
- civilian projects may be delayed;
- imported minerals and components become scarce;
- funding shifts toward secure systems;
- independent firms face consolidation pressure.

Personal changes:

- the player’s work may be reclassified as sensitive;
- Iven faces pressure to limit foreign recruitment;
- Liora may be transferred to emergency operations;
- Tevan may gain status through a restricted program.

Political changes:

- data access and intellectual-property rules tighten;
- foreign professional relationships are reviewed;
- talent retention becomes a national priority.
