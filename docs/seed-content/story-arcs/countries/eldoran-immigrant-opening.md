## Player promise

Eldoran offers opportunity through food, transport, wholesale trade, agricultural services, household markets, and the infrastructure that keeps the entire continent supplied.

The player arrives because Meridian investment is expanding cold storage, rail capacity, wholesale distribution, processing, and food logistics.

The economy looks stable from the outside. Inside, producers, workers, distributors, and households disagree over who should absorb rising costs.

## Arrival scene

The player arrives at Crescent Bay Central Market Terminal before dawn.

Visible signals:

- food shipments moving into wholesale halls;
- rail crews preparing Meridian-linked expansions;
- small merchants purchasing daily inventory;
- public price boards changing by the hour;
- community organizations helping newcomers find rooms and work.

Immediate need:

Choose whether to spend most of the starting balance on stable housing or preserve money for inventory, transportation, or training.

## Initial economic paths

### Food and logistics employment

Work in warehousing, cold storage, rail coordination, inspection support, processing, or distribution.

Strengths:

- reliable demand;
- practical advancement;
- strong connection to essential goods.

Risks:

- physically demanding work;
- thin margins;
- seasonal instability;
- wartime allocation pressure.

### Market trading and small retail

Buy from wholesale suppliers and sell through a stall, neighborhood shop, delivery service, or prepared-food business.

Strengths:

- visible entrepreneurship;
- low starting scale;
- strong community connection.

Risks:

- spoilage;
- price volatility;
- rent and permit costs;
- public criticism during shortages.

### Agricultural and supply analysis

Support forecasting, procurement, reserve planning, transport analysis, or producer services.

Strengths:

- institutional access;
- high learning value;
- influence on major decisions.

Risks:

- uncertain forecasts;
- political pressure;
- conflict between aggregate efficiency and household affordability.

### Local processing enterprise

Build value through preservation, packaging, storage, equipment support, or regional distribution.

Strengths:

- scalable business path;
- reduced waste;
- strong Meridian relevance.

Risks:

- capital requirements;
- energy and equipment dependence;
- concentration risk;
- sensitivity to harvest outcomes.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Mera Dalen

Role: neighborhood market cooperative coordinator

Mera provides the player with a temporary room and access to small market work. She believes newcomers can strengthen local commerce if they are not trapped by exploitative debt.

Expectation:

The player should contribute fairly to the cooperative and avoid profiting through deceptive scarcity claims.

### Local friend: Oren Pell

Role: rail-distribution clerk

Oren understands how ordinary shipping decisions affect food prices. He wants to qualify for operations management but lacks formal credentials.

Conflict:

He supports efficiency but distrusts policies designed entirely by distant institutions.

### Rival peer: Selka Venn

Role: immigrant wholesale trader

Selka is highly disciplined and willing to reinvest nearly everything into growth. She believes scale is the only protection against unstable prices.

Relationship range:

competitor, supplier, emergency partner, or symbol of harmful concentration.

### Gatekeeper: Eren Calder

Existing character: `character.eldoran.eren-calder.v1`

Eren represents household affordability. They may open public-interest Contracts and policy access, but they challenge choices that improve profit while transferring costs to vulnerable households.

## Meridian connection

The player’s first major opportunity comes from Meridian-linked expansion in rail, storage, wholesale distribution, or food-security planning.

Possible hooks:

- cold-storage capacity review;
- household price-impact Contract;
- rail bottleneck assessment;
- cooperative supply bid;
- food-waste reduction project.

## Fracture phase

A weaker harvest forecast raises uncertainty.

Player consequences:

- wholesale prices rise;
- producers demand stronger returns;
- households reduce purchases;
- storage and transport become more valuable;
- Mera faces pressure from struggling members;
- Oren is asked to prioritize politically sensitive cargo;
- Selka expands aggressively and gains market share.

## Attack and war transformation

After the Meridian attack, Eldoran’s food and distribution system becomes strategically critical.

Economic changes:

- reserve policy tightens;
- selected exports may be limited;
- logistics costs rise;
- essential-goods distribution receives priority;
- private storage and processing become politically sensitive.

Personal changes:

- the player may have to choose between premium buyers and local customers;
- Mera’s cooperative may face rationing responsibilities;
- Oren may be transferred into emergency scheduling;
- Selka may secure a major institutional contract that changes local competition.

Political changes:

- food affordability becomes a national-security issue;
- foreign-owned businesses face scrutiny;
- producer and household interests become more polarized.

## Wartime fortune routes

- essential-goods distribution;
- storage and preservation;
- transport coordination;
- regional processing;
- transparent procurement;
- household affordability analysis;
- agricultural recovery and reconstruction.

## Belonging crisis

The player may receive permanent status after becoming essential to the food system.

The opportunity may require accepting a supply arrangement that protects national reserves while reducing support available to someone in the player’s former home.

The player must decide how to balance:

- adopted-country responsibility;
- former-home obligations;
- community affordability;
- business survival;
- personal wealth.
