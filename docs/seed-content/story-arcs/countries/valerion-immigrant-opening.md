## Player promise

Valerion offers opportunity through clean energy, water systems, premium services, tourism, environmental engineering, and green finance.

The player arrives during a Meridian investment wave that promises new infrastructure and international capital.

Valerion is prosperous and attractive, but access to housing, water, and opportunity is unequal. The country’s environmental reputation creates both wealth and political pressure.

## Arrival scene

The player enters Glassfall through a high-speed transit hub overlooking reservoirs, energy infrastructure, and luxury development.

Visible signals:

- green-construction advertisements;
- expensive apartments near the central district;
- workers commuting from outer regions;
- public conservation notices;
- investors promoting Meridian-linked energy projects.

Immediate need:

Choose between affordable housing far from work and expensive housing near the economic center.

## Initial economic paths

### Energy and water infrastructure work

Support operations, maintenance, field services, project administration, or technical documentation.

Strengths:

- stable demand;
- public importance;
- strong Meridian relevance.

Risks:

- credential requirements;
- infrastructure liability;
- political scrutiny;
- wartime allocation pressure.

### Green services enterprise

Build a business in efficiency, maintenance, local transport, tourism support, conservation services, or sustainable supply.

Strengths:

- strong branding environment;
- scalable local demand;
- lower entry capital than major infrastructure.

Risks:

- high rent;
- certification costs;
- dependence on consumer confidence;
- accusations of superficial sustainability.

### Environmental and utility analysis

Work on affordability, water allocation, project review, risk, or public consultation.

Strengths:

- institutional influence;
- public-interest reputation;
- access to policy Contracts.

Risks:

- slow decisions;
- conflicting evidence;
- criticism from both investors and communities.

### Green finance and project support

Enter project evaluation, credit administration, sustainability reporting, or investment analysis.

Strengths:

- high income potential;
- international mobility;
- connection to major projects.

Risks:

- concentration risk;
- reputational exposure;
- conflict between returns and affordability.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Celan Mire

Role: regional settlement and employment coordinator

Celan helps the player find temporary housing and explains the difference between Glassfall’s polished image and its regional inequalities.

Expectation:

The player should complete a community-service or employment commitment tied to the settlement program.

### Local friend: Ressa Vail

Role: utility customer-support specialist

Ressa sees how policy decisions affect ordinary households. She wants to qualify for infrastructure planning but is repeatedly assigned to frontline service work.

Conflict:

She supports conservation but opposes rules that punish households without realistic alternatives.

### Rival peer: Joren Pahl

Role: immigrant green-finance analyst

Joren believes influence comes from serving investors first and improving social outcomes later.

Relationship range:

professional rival, project partner, source of elite access, or public controversy.

### Gatekeeper: Talia Quen

Existing character: `character.valerion.talia-quen.v1`

Talia represents household and regional water access. She can connect the player to public consultations and resilience work, but she challenges projects whose costs fall on ordinary users.

## Meridian connection

The player’s first major opportunity comes from energy, water, conservation, tourism, or financing work connected to Meridian expansion.

Possible hooks:

- reservoir-risk Contract;
- utility-affordability review;
- green-project assessment;
- regional-access consultation;
- efficiency-services business permit.

## Fracture phase

Reservoir levels fall below expectations while international energy commitments expand.

Player consequences:

- conservation services gain demand;
- utility bills and political tension rise;
- investors push for guaranteed exports;
- regional communities demand protection;
- Ressa handles growing household complaints;
- Joren advances by supporting aggressive project finance.

## Attack and war transformation

After the Meridian attack, Valerion’s energy and water systems become strategic assets.

Economic changes:

- energy demand rises;
- tourism and premium services weaken;
- infrastructure security costs increase;
- water allocation becomes more restrictive;
- resilience investment accelerates.

Personal changes:

- the player may lose consumer-facing work but gain strategic infrastructure opportunities;
- Celan faces new displaced arrivals;
- Ressa is assigned to emergency access cases;
- Joren may lead financing for controversial emergency projects.

Political changes:

- export commitments conflict with domestic access;
- emergency conservation rules expand;
- foreign investors face ownership review.

## Wartime fortune routes

- utility resilience;
- water-efficiency services;
- emergency energy coordination;
- regional access planning;
- transparent green-finance review;
- infrastructure repair;
- postwar environmental reconstruction.

## Belonging crisis

The player may receive permanent status through work on a nationally important energy or water project.

The project may improve security while increasing regional prices or limiting access for vulnerable households.

The player must weigh:

- career and status;
- household affordability;
- environmental resilience;
- investor commitments;
- obligations to people outside Valerion.
