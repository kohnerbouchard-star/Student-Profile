## Player promise

Lumenor offers opportunity through education, diplomacy, media, public administration, arbitration, research, and the institutions that coordinate the ten-country world.

The player arrives during the Meridian Forum expansion, when translators, analysts, administrators, educators, service workers, and public-information specialists are in demand.

Lumenor values legitimacy and public trust. It also struggles with slow institutions, expensive living, and the difference between symbolic inclusion and real access.

## Arrival scene

The player arrives at Starfall Civic Transit Hall during preparations for the Meridian Forum.

Visible signals:

- delegates and journalists entering the city;
- universities advertising accelerated programs;
- temporary workers filling hotels and public venues;
- crowded housing outside the diplomatic center;
- public displays explaining the Forum while protesters question its legitimacy.

Immediate need:

Secure a recognized local address required for work, banking, and residency processing.

## Initial economic paths

### Institutional and administrative work

Support records, translation, research, scheduling, public communication, or arbitration services.

Strengths:

- stable reputation;
- broad professional network;
- direct Meridian access.

Risks:

- slow advancement;
- temporary contracts;
- political neutrality pressure;
- dependence on public funding.

### Education and training

Work as a tutor, program assistant, curriculum specialist, technical trainer, or student worker.

Strengths:

- accessible community relationships;
- transferable skills;
- long-term social influence.

Risks:

- modest early income;
- credential barriers;
- unstable funding during crisis.

### Media and public information

Enter reporting support, verification, research, editing, translation, or data presentation.

Strengths:

- connection to major events;
- public-interest reputation;
- access to diverse institutions.

Risks:

- political pressure;
- incomplete evidence;
- correction obligations;
- personal scrutiny during war.

### Forum-services enterprise

Build a business in lodging, food, event support, translation, professional services, or civic technology.

Strengths:

- strong boom demand;
- entrepreneurial flexibility;
- international client base.

Risks:

- high rent;
- seasonal demand;
- dependence on diplomatic activity;
- rapid decline if the Forum collapses.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Nela Corin

Role: community settlement officer

Nela helps the player secure temporary housing and navigate Starfall’s public systems. She believes Lumenor’s reputation depends on whether institutions work for ordinary newcomers, not only delegates.

Expectation:

The player should complete required appointments and contribute to a settlement or language-support program.

### Local friend: Arven Lis

Role: junior public-records researcher

Arven is curious, idealistic, and frustrated by how slowly institutions correct obvious problems.

Goal:

Become an independent investigator without losing access to public records.

### Rival peer: Mirael Doss

Role: immigrant policy fellow

Mirael is skilled at institutional language and believes influence requires patience, discretion, and proximity to power.

Relationship range:

mentor-like rival, collaborator, gatekeeper, or symbol of compromise.

### Gatekeeper: Dena Holt

Existing character: `character.lumenor.dena-holt.v1`

Dena represents public-interest verification and correction. She can connect the player to reporting and evidence work, but she expects clear separation between fact, allegation, forecast, and opinion.

## Meridian connection

The player’s first major opportunity comes from Forum preparation, public communication, translation, research, education, or services.

Possible hooks:

- country-briefing Contract;

- Forum logistics assignment;
- community-impact review;

## Fracture phase

The Forum becomes more hostile as countries dispute ownership, security, and responsibility.

Player consequences:

- institutional work expands but becomes politically sensitive;
- housing costs rise near the diplomatic district;
- public demonstrations affect ordinary commerce;
- people demand certainty before evidence is complete;
- Arven uncovers contradictory records;
- Mirael gains influence by controlling access and language.

## Attack and war transformation

After the Meridian attack, Lumenor becomes a center for diplomacy, refugees, public information, and emergency coordination.

Economic changes:

- diplomatic and administrative demand rises;
- tourism and ordinary education may decline;
- housing and public-service pressure increase;
- verification and mediation become more valuable;
- public budgets shift toward crisis response.

Personal changes:

- the player may be asked to translate, verify, or communicate information with major consequences;
- Nela receives difficult settlement cases;
- Arven may possess evidence that powerful institutions want delayed;
- Mirael may enter an emergency policy office.

Political changes:

- neutrality becomes difficult to maintain;
- public trust depends on corrections and transparent evidence;
- residency systems face pressure from displacement and security concerns.

## Wartime fortune routes

- public-information verification;
- translation and mediation;
- settlement and housing coordination;
- emergency administration;
- education continuity;
- arbitration and claims support;
- postwar institutional reconstruction.

## Belonging crisis

The player may receive permanent status through public or diplomatic service.

The opportunity may require discretion about evidence, relationships, or failures that the player believes the public should know.

The player must decide whether belonging means:

- protecting institutional stability;
- preserving independent truth;
- serving displaced communities;
- remaining neutral;
- or using access to pursue personal advancement.
