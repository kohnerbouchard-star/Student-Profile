## Purpose

This directory contains the country-specific beginning of the standard Econovaria campaign.

Every opening starts from the same core condition:

- the player is a new immigrant;
- the player has temporary status and limited funds;
- the Meridian boom creates a credible path toward wealth;
- the adopted country provides distinct opportunities and constraints;
- international fracture later changes the player’s work, relationships, status, and obligations;
- the player experiences war primarily through civilian and economic consequences.

## Shared relationship model

Each opening contains:

- a sponsor or settlement contact;
- a local friend with an independent goal;
- an immigrant peer or rival using a different strategy;
- an existing institutional character who controls access or accountability;
- an unresolved relationship to the player’s former home.

These are reusable character definitions and relationship roles only. A future runtime must create player-specific relationship instances.

## Shared story rhythm

1. Arrival and immediate need.
2. Basic stabilization.
3. Choice among several economic paths.
4. Connection to the Meridian boom.
5. International fracture reaches daily life.
6. The Meridian attack changes established plans.
7. The player adapts economically during war.
8. Residency, loyalty, and belonging become personal conflicts.
9. Personal and world outcomes are evaluated separately.

## Comparison dimensions

Reviewers should compare the openings across:

- ease of early employment;
- capital requirements;
- housing pressure;
- public-service access;
- exposure to trade disruption;
- strategic-industry importance;
- residency pressure;
- relationship stakes;
- wartime opportunity;
- risk of one dominant strategy;
- distinctiveness of the adopted-country experience.

## Current limitations

The files do not establish:

- exact starting balances;
- approved rewards or prices;
- implemented residency mechanics;
- implemented relationship persistence;
- implemented housing or ordinary-expense systems;
- implemented world-phase or war-state storage;
- executable Contracts or events;
- production-approved names.

All numerical tuning requires an actual executable simulation and staging validation.
