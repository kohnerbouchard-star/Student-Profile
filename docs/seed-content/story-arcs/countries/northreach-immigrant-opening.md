Version: 1.0.0-draft

## Player promise

Northreach offers high wages, scarce skills, strategic industries, and rapid advancement to people willing to endure distance, cold, regulation, and political suspicion.

The player arrives because the Meridian boom has increased demand for mineral processing, energy logistics, infrastructure maintenance, engineering support, and northern transport.

The country appears wealthy and disciplined. It is also deeply concerned about foreign ownership and dependence.

## Arrival scene

The player arrives at Frostgate Northern Terminal during a controlled intake period for skilled workers and project personnel.

Visible signals:

- mineral freight moving under heavy security;
- employers recruiting technical and logistics workers;
- expensive housing near transport links;
- public notices about strategic-industry disclosure;
- political advertisements warning against foreign control.

Immediate need:

Secure temporary housing before the employer accommodation window closes.

## Initial economic paths

### Strategic-industry employment

Work for a mineral, energy, transport, or infrastructure operator.

Strengths:

- stable income;
- training;
- access to Meridian-linked work;
- strong advancement potential.

Risks:

- employer dependence;
- security reviews;
- limited mobility;
- wartime strategic obligations.

### Northern logistics services

Build income through freight coordination, equipment support, translation, maintenance scheduling, or cold-region services.

Strengths:

- lower entry capital;
- broad client base;
- independent reputation growth.

Risks:

- weather and route disruption;
- payment delays;
- insurance exposure;
- reliance on imported equipment.

### Resource-market analysis

Use research, finance, or trading skills to follow mineral, energy, and transport demand.

Strengths:

- high upside;
- strong connection to global events;
- access to institutional Contracts.

Risks:

- volatility;
- information uncertainty;
- public suspicion of speculative profit during shortages.

### Local supply business

Provide housing, food, repair, training, or ordinary services to new workers.

Strengths:

- direct connection to migration growth;
- visible community impact;
- scalable local demand.

Risks:

- high operating costs;
- rent pressure;
- seasonal demand;
- household affordability criticism.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Edda Veyr

Role: workforce-settlement coordinator

Edda helped arrange the player’s first documents and housing lead. She believes immigration is necessary for growth but fears public backlash if employers exploit new arrivals.

Expectation:

The player must complete required reporting and avoid creating problems that threaten the settlement program.

### Local friend: Jonis Hale

Role: equipment technician

Jonis introduces the player to Frostgate’s ordinary working life. He wants to save enough to leave rotational work and open a repair shop.

Conflict:

He welcomes the player but resents employers who use immigrant recruitment to avoid improving conditions.

### Rival peer: Mares Kovan

Role: newly arrived mineral-market analyst

Mares believes rapid wealth is the only real protection an immigrant can obtain. He takes greater risks and treats institutional loyalty as temporary.

Relationship range:

competitor, collaborator, warning example, or eventual business partner.

### Gatekeeper: Rian Kest

Existing character: `character.northreach.rian-kest.v1`

Rian represents labor and worker interests. He can help the player access fairer employment channels, but he expects solidarity when conditions deteriorate.

Possible hooks:

## Fracture phase

Northreach begins reviewing strategic exports.

Player consequences:

- overtime and wages may rise;
- imported equipment becomes more expensive;
- foreign workers face additional disclosure checks;
- market opportunities expand while public suspicion increases;
- Jonis fears unsafe production pressure;
- Mares profits from volatility and urges the player to move faster.

## Attack and war transformation

After the Meridian attack, Northreach treats mineral, energy, and rail systems as national-security assets.

Economic changes:

- strategic demand rises;
- selected exports are restricted;
- civilian imports become more expensive;
- industrial employment expands;
- housing near secure facilities tightens.

Personal changes:

- the player’s status may become tied to an employer or sector;
- Edda faces pressure to reduce new-worker intake;
- Jonis may be assigned to emergency maintenance;
- Mares may be investigated after aggressive market activity.

Political changes:

- loyalty and disclosure requirements expand;
- foreign ownership becomes controversial;
- worker protections and production demands collide.

## Wartime fortune routes

- worker housing and essential services;

## Belonging crisis

The player may be offered long-term status through strategic employment.

The offer may require:

- extended service in a controlled industry;
- disclosure of foreign financial relationships;
- restrictions on selected travel or contracts;
- public support for a policy the player considers unfair.

At the same time, someone from the player’s former home may ask for help obtaining scarce Northreach materials or financial support.
