## Player promise

Yrethia offers access to the most trusted shipping, customs, insurance, and freight-finance system in Econovaria.

The player arrives during a logistics boom. New cargo, new capital, and new workers are entering Sableport faster than the city can comfortably absorb them.

Success is possible, but reputation matters. One error can close doors across the entire maritime economy.

## Arrival scene

The player enters through Sableport International Passenger and Labor Terminal.

Visible signals:

- ships waiting beyond the harbor;
- customs queues moving under strict documentation rules;
- logistics firms recruiting multilingual staff;
- premium offices beside crowded worker housing;
- insurance notices warning about capacity and cyber risk.

Immediate need:

Resolve a mismatch between the player’s temporary address and employment documents before their first work appointment.

## Initial economic paths

### Customs and compliance work

Support cargo records, translation, documentation, inspection scheduling, or trade verification.

Strengths:

- stable career ladder;
- strong institutional reputation;
- direct Meridian relevance.

Risks:

- strict accountability;
- slow advancement without credentials;
- personal exposure when records are disputed.

### Freight and port operations

Work in scheduling, warehousing, dispatch, cargo planning, or service coordination.

Strengths:

- immediate demand;
- practical advancement;
- strong overtime potential.

Risks:

- congestion;
- injury and fatigue pressure;
- automation anxiety;
- wartime route disruption.

### Insurance and freight finance

Enter underwriting support, claims review, risk analysis, or trade-credit administration.

Strengths:

- high-skill income;
- access to market intelligence;
- portable professional reputation.

Risks:

- complex entry requirements;
- moral pressure over denied claims;
- sensitivity to conflict and fraud allegations.

### Small port-services business

Offer food, transport, lodging, document support, equipment rental, or maintenance to port workers and travelers.

Strengths:

- direct demand from the boom;
- community visibility;
- lower formal credential barrier.

Risks:

- high rent;
- permit requirements;
- dependence on port traffic;
- vulnerability to shutdowns.

## Personal relationship anchors

### Sponsor: Leva Orren

Role: licensed settlement and documentation adviser

Leva helped the player complete the legal arrival process. She believes rules protect newcomers from exploitation, but she also knows the system can become inaccessible to people without money or fluent language skills.

Expectation:

The player should protect their record and avoid shortcuts that could jeopardize Leva’s license.

### Local friend: Perran Dey

Role: night-shift cargo scheduler

Perran teaches the player how Sableport actually works after official offices close. He wants to earn enough to move his family away from the congested harbor district.

Conflict:

He values safety but fears stricter compliance will eliminate smaller operators and jobs.

### Rival peer: Alin Sorev

Role: immigrant insurance trainee

Alin is disciplined, competitive, and convinced that perfect conformity is the safest path to belonging.

Relationship range:

study partner, career rival, defender, or person who reports concerns when trust breaks.

### Gatekeeper: Nadi Oran

Existing character: `character.yrethia.nadi-oran.v1`

Nadi represents dockworkers and logistics labor. She can open practical work and community connections, but she challenges decisions that shift risk onto workers.

## Fracture phase

Port capacity tightens and international cargo records become politically sensitive.

Player consequences:

- wages and overtime may rise;
- housing and transport costs increase;
- insurers raise documentation requirements;
- smaller clients struggle to comply;
- foreign-language mistakes attract disproportionate scrutiny;
- Perran is pressured to move cargo faster;
- Alin gains advancement by supporting stricter standards.

## Attack and war transformation

The Meridian attack damages trust in cargo and payment records.

Economic changes:

- shipping premiums rise;
- selected routes close;
- manual verification slows trade;
- warehousing demand grows;
- claims and disputes increase.

Personal changes:

- the player’s professional record becomes critical;
- Leva faces emergency residency cases;
- Perran may lose work if a terminal closes;
- Alin may receive authority over high-stakes reviews.

Political changes:

- foreign cargo and workers face additional screening;
- emergency powers challenge normal commercial law;
- pressure grows to exclude Thalorian routes or Syndalian systems.

The player must weigh:

- community trust;
