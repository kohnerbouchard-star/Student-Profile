# Meridian Corridor Classroom Operation

Stable content ID: `operation-model.global.meridian-classroom.v1`
Content type: classroom scenario operation model
Version: 1.0.0-draft
Maturity: draft
Applies to: `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`
Implementation status: manual-compatible

## Purpose

Define a classroom-operable sequence for the Meridian pilot that controls cognitive load, instructor review burden, timing, group decisions, and narrative pacing.

## Recommended pilot length

Recommended full pilot: 5 class periods of approximately 45–55 minutes.

Condensed option: 3 periods by assigning country analysis as homework and combining disruption response with outcome reflection.

Extended option: 7–8 periods with company analysis, market observation, formal negotiation, and revised policy submissions.

## Recommended five-period sequence

## Period 1: World and Corridor introduction

Student work:

- country briefing;
- global overview;
- `contract.meridian.evaluate-corridor.v1`.

Target student work time:

- briefing: 8–10 minutes;
- contract: 15–20 minutes;
- discussion: 15 minutes.

Instructor work:

- release the arc;
- answer terminology questions;
- use fast rubric for the first contract;
- avoid activating capacity warnings.

Cognitive-load rule:

One global proposal, no active crisis.

## Period 2: Country interests and dependencies

Student work:

- `contract.meridian.analyze-country-exposure.v1`;
- country-team comparison;
- initial country condition.

Target student work time:

- profile review: 8 minutes;
- contract: 20–25 minutes;
- team synthesis: 10–15 minutes.

Instructor work:

- confirm country assignment;
- monitor country-specific evidence;
- approve or request focused revision.

Cognitive-load rule:

One country profile plus one comparison country.

## Period 3: Financing and governance

Student work:

- four-model comparison;
- `contract.meridian.compare-financing-governance.v1`;
- country-team recommendation.

Target student work time:

- model review: 10–12 minutes;
- structured comparison: 20–25 minutes;
- country-team decision: 10 minutes.

Instructor work:

- ensure no model is presented as costless;
- record country-team recommendations;
- do not resolve the Corridor yet.

Cognitive-load rule:

Use a structured comparison matrix. Do not add a capacity warning until recommendations are recorded.

## Period 4: Disruption and emergency decision

Student work:

- receive one primary warning relevant to country or team;
- receive shared security event later in the period or next segment;
- complete `contract.meridian.respond-first-disruption.v1`;
- participate in declared emergency-decision method.

Target student work time:

- warning and evidence: 5–8 minutes;
- response: 15–20 minutes;
- emergency council: 15 minutes.

Instructor work:

- activate no more than two concurrent capacity warnings in the standard profile;
- activate only one primary action request per player at a time;
- explain the published no-response default before the deadline;
- record selected security response.

Cognitive-load rule:

Maximum two unresolved capacity warnings globally before the security event. Each player receives one country-relevant warning plus the shared security context.

## Period 5: Resolution and reflection

Student work:

- review actual resolution evidence;
- compare predicted and observed effects;
- complete `contract.meridian.review-outcome.v1`;
- identify one follow-up recommendation.

Target student work time:

- outcome review: 8–10 minutes;
- reflection contract: 20 minutes;
- discussion: 15–20 minutes.

Instructor work:

- apply `resolution-model.global.meridian-corridor.v1`;
- record the decisive conditions;
- release a player-facing resolution explanation;
- complete chain approval without duplicate rewards.

## Classroom contribution modes

Select one before Period 1.

### Individual recommendation mode

Use when:

- class is small;
- country teams are uneven;
- the objective is individual reasoning.

Behavior:

- each player submits independent work;
- instructor records national positions separately or uses a preconfigured scenario;
- individual recommendations do not automatically become national policy.

### Country-team mode — recommended pilot default

Use when:

- several students share each country;
- collaboration and negotiation are learning objectives.

Behavior:

- individual contracts remain individual unless explicitly designated group work;
- each country team produces one official condition and governance recommendation;
- the team selects one spokesperson or submits a structured country form;
- instructor records the country position;
- minority reasoning can remain in individual reflections.

### Classroom council mode

Use when:

- the class can sustain a formal negotiation;
- instructor can manage voting or consensus.

Behavior:

- voting rules are announced before debate;
- abstention and no-response rules are explicit;
- instructor records the final decisions;
- the runtime does not imply an automatic vote unless supported.

## Standard-profile event limits

- Maximum unresolved capacity warnings before security crisis: 2.
- Maximum major global crises at once: 1.
- Maximum player-facing action requests at once: 1 primary plus 1 optional informational task.
- Minimum time between major stage introductions: enough to read the prior outcome and complete required work.
- A correction does not count as a new crisis but must be clearly linked to the prior forecast.

## Instructor review budget

Target review time per individual submission:

- Evaluate Corridor: 60–90 seconds using four-point fast rubric.
- Country Exposure: 90–120 seconds.
- Financing and Governance: 2–3 minutes.
- Disruption Response: 90–120 seconds.
- Outcome Review: 90–120 seconds.

Recommended controls:

- structured response fields;
- required evidence checkboxes or references;
- rubric categories rather than open-ended prose grading;
- changes-requested templates;
- batch approval only where the same rubric conditions are clearly satisfied;
- no automated semantic approval unless the implementation is reviewed separately.

## Contract response-length guidance

- Evaluate Corridor: 180–300 words or equivalent structured fields.
- Country Exposure: 220–350 words.
- Financing and Governance: structured matrix plus 200–300 word recommendation.
- Disruption Response: 180–300 words.
- Outcome Review: 220–350 words.

Alternative formats may include teacher-approved presentation, worksheet, or group memorandum, but the evidence requirements remain the same.

## Prerequisite instruction

Before the pilot, students should understand:

- scarcity;
- opportunity cost;
- basic supply and demand;
- trade and specialization;
- inflation;
- simple interest and debt concepts;
- risk and uncertainty.

Concepts introduced during the pilot:

- infrastructure governance;
- transaction costs;
- externalities;
- concentration risk;
- information and trust;
- delayed consequences.

## Accessibility requirements

- all maps have a list alternative;
- country and model summaries use headings and short paragraphs;
- no response depends on color alone;
- response deadlines are stated in text;
- character messages identify speaker and institution;
- long briefings include a short summary;
- keyboard completion path exists;
- screen-reader labels identify current stage and active warning;
- reduced-motion settings do not hide event state changes;
- students may submit an equivalent structured response where long-form writing is not the learning objective.

## Late, revision, and expiry policy

Recommended standard profile:

- accepted work receives a short grace window after new acceptance closes;
- changes requested preserve the original submission and review history;
- a revised submission does not create a second reward;
- an expired unaccepted contract creates no monetary penalty;
- a crisis response submitted after the decision resolves may be accepted as reflection but cannot alter the resolved state;
- closed-session late work follows instructor policy and must not duplicate chain completion.

## Student information panel

Every active task should expose:

- current story stage;
- confirmed facts;
- forecasts and uncertainties;
- assigned country;
- relevant countries and institutions;
- active deadline;
- required output;
- reward status;
- glossary links;
- how the response will be used.

## Instructor resolution checklist

Before selecting a terminal outcome:

- country contribution mode confirmed;
- participating countries recorded;
- financing status recorded;
- governance status recorded;
- physical readiness recorded;
- security response and integrity status recorded;
- safeguards recorded;
- hard blockers evaluated;
- decisive condition documented;
- player-facing explanation prepared.

## Validation

- declared classroom mode does not change mid-arc without explanation;
- event limits enforced manually or mechanically;
- no player receives two simultaneous required crisis responses;
- review time measured during staging;
- rubric files exist for every pilot contract;
- late and revision behavior matches backend lifecycle;
- resolution explanation protects private submissions;
- chain completion and rewards remain idempotent.
