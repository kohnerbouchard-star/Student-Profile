Version: 1.0.0-draft
Maturity: draft
Applies to: `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`
Implementation status: content and continuity specification only

## Purpose

Define how recurring characters respond to the four terminal Meridian outcomes so follow-up dialogue remains consistent with prior priorities, accepted costs, authority, and evidence.

This matrix does not create economic effects or character-relationship state by itself.

## Reaction-writing rules

1. A character may accept an outcome they did not prefer when minimum conditions are credible.
2. No character treats one resolution as universally good or bad.
3. Immediate reactions distinguish confirmed outcome from expected effects.
4. Characters acknowledge the cost accepted by their own preferred model.
5. Supporting characters may challenge their country’s lead institution.
6. Reactions never reveal private player submissions.
7. Corrections or revised outcomes require a new reaction record rather than silent replacement.

## Follow-up interaction requirements

Every terminal resolution should generate:

- one lead Forum explanation;
- one country-specific lead reaction for the player’s assigned country;
- one supporting or independent reaction when the outcome creates a material distributional, labor, accountability, rights, or public-information issue;
- one company or market analysis where relevant;
- the outcome-review Contract;
- no more than one required follow-up action per player at a time.

## Conditional reaction modifiers

A reaction may vary based on recorded conditions.

Examples:

- Cassian Rhyl reacts more cautiously when financing is shared rather than Xalvorian-led.
- Elia Varen reacts more positively when water and environmental safeguards are binding.
- Neris Vale reacts more negatively when access is incomplete or manual verification remains widespread.
- Tovan Rell reacts more negatively when Thaloris is excluded despite successful audit evidence.
- Eren Vale reacts more negatively when household protections are absent.
- Rian Kest and Ilya Dren react more negatively when production commitments lack funded staffing or transition terms.

These variations require authoritative condition state or an instructor-selected content variant. They must not infer hidden conditions from player text alone.

## Disagreement rules

- A supporting character may publicly disagree with a lead character without creating an institutional split unless the story state records one.
- Disagreement should name the evidence or cost at issue.
- Characters do not become hostile merely because a player selected another model.
- Repeated ignored concerns may change future trust or willingness only when a relationship model exists.
- Without a relationship model, disagreement remains narrative copy and future content selection metadata.

## Correction behavior

If the resolution is revised:

- preserve the original reactions;
- issue updated reactions only from materially affected characters;
- identify what evidence changed;
- do not imply the character had predicted the corrected outcome unless prior copy supports that claim.

## Validation

- reactions match character priorities and knowledge limits;
- all four outcomes retain both benefits and costs;
- no private player response exposed;
- supporting perspectives remain visible;
- reaction text cannot create debt, access, ownership, rewards, or economic effects;
- runtime variants require recorded conditions;
- one player action cap preserved;
- renamed characters must update this matrix before canon approval.
