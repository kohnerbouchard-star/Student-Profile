## Headline

Participating Countries Approve Shared Meridian Charter and Oversight

## Summary

The Meridian Corridor will proceed under a multilateral charter with distributed financing, shared rules, and binding oversight. The agreement reduces concentrated control but will require slower coordination and continuing negotiation.

## Article

Participating countries have approved a shared Meridian charter after reaching minimum conditions for broad participation, financing, physical readiness, data integrity, and safeguards.

The agreement creates a multilateral governance structure. The final session record must identify voting or decision rules, emergency authority, review institutions, financing contributors, and the limits placed on lead operators.

Supporters argue that the charter distributes risk and reduces dependence on one country, lender, route, or platform. Critics warn that shared authority can delay construction, increase administrative cost, and make responsibility harder to identify when problems occur.

The multilateral structure does not eliminate disagreement. Countries retain different interests regarding resources, ports, food, water, finance, industrial orders, and data access. The charter establishes how those disagreements will be reviewed rather than assuming they will disappear.
