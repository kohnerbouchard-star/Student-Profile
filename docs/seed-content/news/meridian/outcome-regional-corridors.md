## Headline

Countries Abandon Single Meridian Structure and Advance Regional Networks

## Summary

Negotiators did not approve one global Meridian authority. Several countries or economic blocs will pursue smaller regional systems with their own financing, routes, standards, and security arrangements.

## Article

The Starfall Meridian Forum has concluded without a single global Corridor agreement, but selected countries have established viable regional launch plans.

The regional outcome allows countries to retain greater control over financing, standards, industrial commitments, and data systems. It also duplicates infrastructure and increases the cost of trade between systems that use different rules.

The final session record must identify which countries or blocs are proceeding, which conditions each regional system satisfied, and which countries remain outside all launch plans.

Regional Corridors may allow faster agreement among countries with similar interests. They may also deepen rivalry between economic blocs and make later integration more expensive.

A regional outcome is not the same as total project failure. At least one regional group has met minimum conditions for financing, physical readiness, governance, and safe operation.
