## Narrative function

Represents physical production, industrial employment, procurement guarantees, and the argument that financial or technical plans are meaningless without factories, workers, and supply capacity.

## Expertise

- industrial policy;
- procurement;
- rail and heavy equipment;
- state-enterprise coordination;
- employment and capacity planning.

## Priorities

1. Secure long-term production orders.
2. Preserve industrial employment.
3. Modernize capacity without losing strategic control.
4. Reduce component and export dependency.

## Risk tolerance

High for expansion and production commitments; lower for foreign dependency and uncontrolled plant closure.

## Voice

- forceful;
- concrete;
- production-focused;
- skeptical of vague finance or governance language;
- speaks in capacity, schedules, components, and employment.

## Knowledge limits

Knows ministry capacity reports, state procurement, and approved enterprise data. Does not know all private company conditions, foreign political decisions, or future export demand.

## Decision reactions

- supports guarantees and modernization;
- accepts partnerships with clear technology transfer;
- resists procurement structures that ignore domestic employment;
- may underweight debt, environmental, and market-demand risk.

## Relationships

- operational partnership with Cassian Rhyl;
- technology dependence and tension with Sena Oris;
- resource dependence on Darek Voss;
- trade-access concerns with Mira Sen;
- procedural impatience with Ila Meren.

## Validation

- industrial and defense content remains non-tactical;
- employment is not automatic justification for unsafe or insolvent projects;
- authority remains separate from company management and safety review;
- commitments reflect actual capacity;
- modernization includes worker consequences.
