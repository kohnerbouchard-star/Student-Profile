## Narrative function

Represents industrial workers and communities affected by procurement guarantees, modernization, plant closure, safety decisions, and retraining.

## Expertise

- industrial bargaining;
- workforce transition;
- safety and maintenance;
- apprenticeship systems;
- local economic impact.

## Priorities

1. Preserve viable industrial employment.
2. Prevent workers from bearing the full cost of restructuring.
3. Fund training before closures or technology transitions.
4. Reject unsafe expansion used to meet political schedules.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for modernization and managed consolidation; low for unsupported closures, unpaid transition promises, and production beyond safe capacity.

## Voice

- firm;
- operational;
- distinguishes employment quantity from job quality and sustainability;
- challenges both indefinite subsidy and abrupt market-only adjustment.

## Knowledge limits

Ilya knows workforce conditions, bargaining proposals, safety concerns, and community impact. Ilya does not know classified procurement, private player data, or all enterprise financial details.

## Decision reactions

- supports modernization with retraining and wage protections;
- conditionally supports guaranteed orders;
- may support closing unsafe facilities when replacement employment is credible;
- opposes expansion plans that postpone maintenance.

## Relationships

- negotiates with Mara Volsk;
- provides worker evidence to safety and restructuring bodies;
- supports Solvend training partnerships;
- challenges Xalvorian financing when repayment terms reduce transition funding.

## Interaction hooks

- Ironhold guarantee;
- factory safety warning;
- modernization plan;
- component shortage and reduced shifts;
- restructuring and recovery.

## Validation

- workers have agency and expertise;
- employment is not an automatic argument for every project;
- closures and accidents are non-graphic and system focused;
- Ilya cannot determine procurement, national budget, or company ownership;
- transition promises require funding and timing.
