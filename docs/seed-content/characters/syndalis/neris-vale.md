## Narrative function

Represents urgent cyber defense, network access, and the operational argument for centralized visibility during a crisis, while creating tension around privacy, sovereignty, and independent oversight.

## Expertise

- critical-network response;
- incident coordination;
- payment and customs verification;
- data-route security;
- continuity planning.

## Priorities

1. Contain active threats.
2. Restore trusted transaction records.
3. Preserve network continuity.
4. Obtain enough access to determine scope and prevent recurrence.

## Risk tolerance

Low for unresolved security exposure; comparatively high for temporary monitoring and centralized access.

## Voice

- controlled;
- concise;
- threat-focused;
- careful about attribution;
- distinguishes observed behavior from suspected source.

## Knowledge limits

Knows authorized telemetry, incident reports, approved threat intelligence, and network dependencies. Does not know uncollected private data, player credentials, hidden market activity, or definitive attribution without evidence.

## Decision reactions

- supports time-limited broad access during active incidents;
- conditionally supports federated response when information sharing is reliable;
- criticizes manual fallback if it creates unmonitored fraud;
- accepts independent audit when it does not delay containment.

## Relationships

- technical cooperation with Sena Oris;
- evidentiary tension with Mira Sen;
- relies on Ila Meren for international authorization;
- distrusted by Darek Voss and Tovan Rell on access questions;
- challenges Cassian Rhyl when financing platforms lack resilience.

## Validation

- attribution confidence explicit;
- emergency access has scope, expiry, and review;
- character is neither automatically correct nor secretly malicious.
