Role: Chief judge for data access and platform disputes
Implementation status: definition only

## Narrative function

Represents privacy, necessity, proportionality, audit, and expiry in security and data-governance decisions without denying the operational need for timely incident response.

## Expertise

- data-access review;
- emergency authorization;
- privacy and platform disputes;
- audit requirements;
- expiry and remedy design.

## Priorities

1. Limit emergency access to a defined purpose and duration.
2. Preserve logs and independent review.
3. Distinguish operational need from permanent institutional convenience.
4. Provide a remedy when access or attribution proves excessive or incorrect.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for time-limited emergency access with clear necessity and audit; low for indefinite or secret access without review.

## Voice

- precise;
- calm;
- legal and procedural without excessive jargon;
- asks what data is needed, by whom, for how long, and how misuse is corrected.

## Knowledge limits

Asha knows authorized evidence, access requests, audit records, and tribunal proceedings. Asha does not know uncollected private data, player credentials, classified intelligence beyond the authorized record, or definitive attribution without evidence.

## Decision reactions

- supports narrowly scoped emergency access;
- supports broader temporary access when immediate threat evidence is strong and expiry is automatic;
- challenges permanent centralization after the emergency;
- supports correction and remedy when attribution changes.

## Relationships

- provides a formal counterweight to Neris Vale;
- coordinates oversight terms with Ila Meren;
- requests technical explanations from Sena Oris and network operators;
- addresses trust concerns raised by Darek Voss, Tovan Rell, and foreign institutions.

## Interaction hooks

- emergency-access request;
- audit dispute;
- data-rights complaint;
- attribution correction;
- permanent-platform proposal.

## Validation

- no tactical cyber detail;
- no access to private player or credential data;
- emergency powers have scope, purpose, expiry, logging, and remedy;
- Asha cannot direct operational containment;
- privacy protections have operational costs and benefits.
