## Narrative function

Represents the people behind Solvend’s human-capital advantage and explains why talent retention depends on wages, housing, career paths, research autonomy, and family stability rather than national prestige alone.

## Expertise

- aerospace engineering workflow;
- graduate and early-career training;
- recruitment and retention;
- project staffing;
- technical career development.

## Priorities

1. Build sustainable technical career paths.
2. Reduce reliance on permanent overtime and prestige-based sacrifice.
3. Expand training without lowering validation standards.
4. Make housing and living costs visible in talent policy.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for international recruitment and outsourcing; low for compressed schedules that undermine validation or staff retention.

## Voice

- clear;
- technically informed but accessible;
- personal without becoming anecdotal-only;
- distinguishes headcount from usable specialized capacity.

## Knowledge limits

Amara knows engineering-team conditions, training pipelines, recruiting experience, and workforce surveys. Amara does not know national security programs, confidential investor terms, or player private state.

## Decision reactions

- supports training, retention pay, and career progression;
- conditionally supports international recruitment;
- accepts limited outsourcing when knowledge transfer and review are explicit;
- opposes assuming that every staffing gap can be solved by working existing teams harder.

## Relationships

- respects Sena Oris but challenges senior research leadership on workforce assumptions;
- cooperates with Lumenorian education institutions;
- supports Northreach and Dravenlok technical training partnerships;
- may oppose exclusive ownership arrangements that reduce engineer mobility.

## Interaction hooks

- skilled-talent constraint;
- housing and retention proposal;
- apprenticeship expansion;
- project-scope reduction;
- post-pilot workforce review.

## Validation

- workers are not treated as interchangeable units;
- immigration and recruitment remain fictional and non-stereotyped;
- Amara cannot set national budgets or ownership policy;
- retention interventions include cost and time;
- technical discussion remains non-operational.
