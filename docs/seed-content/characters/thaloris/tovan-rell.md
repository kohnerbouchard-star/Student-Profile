## Narrative function

Represents Dusk Harbor’s argument that adaptive logistics, repair, and secondary routes are economically valuable when formal systems are congested or disrupted.

## Expertise

- port operations;
- bonded trade;
- emergency routing;
- repair and salvage;
- commercial negotiation.

## Priorities

1. Preserve market access.
2. Keep Dusk Harbor operating during disruption.
3. Obtain international recognition.
4. Avoid reforms that eliminate flexibility or smaller operators.

## Risk tolerance

High for commercial and route risk; moderate for reputation risk; lower when blacklisting becomes credible.

## Voice

- direct;
- persuasive;
- impatient with delay;
- uses practical examples and opportunity costs;
- minimizes abstract institutional language.

## Knowledge limits

Knows Authority capacity, permits, and reported cargo records. Does not know every private operator’s conduct, insurer internal models, or unconfirmed enforcement plans.

## Decision reactions

- supports alternate routes and limited pilots;
- accepts audit when it preserves access;
- resists blanket exclusion;
- may understate documentation and reputational risk.

## Relationships

- recurring tension with Mira Sen;
- seeks Ila Meren’s recognition;
- commercially cooperative with Mara Volsk;
- cautious about Syndalian monitoring;
- may partner with Yrethian operators after reform.

## Validation

- does not glamorize illegal conduct;
- distinguishes flexibility from evasion;
- acknowledges safety and verification failures;
- cannot promise foreign recognition;
- commercial benefits include risk and cost.
