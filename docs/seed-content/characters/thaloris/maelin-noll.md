# Maelin Noll

Stable content ID: `character.thaloris.maelin-noll.v1`
Content type: recurring supporting character
Version: 1.0.0-draft
Maturity: draft
Country: Thaloris
Institution: Thaloris Trade Legitimacy Commission — `institution.thaloris.trade-legitimacy-commission.v1`
Role: Commission chair
Implementation status: definition only
Pronunciation: MAY-lin NOHL

## Narrative function

Represents domestic reformers who want Thaloris to gain durable access to regulated trade without allowing external institutions to define every flexible or secondary-market activity as illegitimate.

## Expertise

- trade compliance;
- public audit;
- customs reform;
- commercial recognition;
- institutional accountability.

## Priorities

1. Improve the credibility and auditability of Dusk Harbor commerce.
2. Preserve legitimate repair, salvage, re-export, and secondary-market activity.
3. Create proportional standards rather than blanket exclusion.
4. Prevent reform from becoming a tool for incumbent concentration.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for phased reform and independent audit; low for concealment, indefinite exceptions, and politically selective enforcement.

## Voice

- precise;
- reform-oriented;
- distinguishes informal, unregulated, noncompliant, and illegal activity;
- rejects both romanticization and blanket condemnation.

## Knowledge limits

Maelin knows Commission reviews, public audit results, operator disclosures, and recognition negotiations. Maelin does not know undisclosed private cargo, foreign intelligence, or player private state.

## Decision reactions

- supports limited audited pilots;
- supports replacing operators after confirmed serious violations;
- opposes public accusations before evidence is reviewed;
- resists standards that smaller firms cannot meet without transition support.

## Relationships

- challenges Tovan Rell when speed outruns verification;
- negotiates with Mira Sen and Yrethian customs institutions;
- seeks procedural support from Ila Meren;
- works with repair and warehouse groups on practical compliance.

## Interaction hooks

- Dusk Harbor audit;
- operator disclosure dispute;
- route-recognition negotiation;
- correction after an unsupported allegation;
- reform backlash.

## Validation

- reform has economic costs and benefits;
- allegations retain fact status and due process;
- Thaloris is not reduced to criminality;
- Maelin cannot guarantee foreign recognition;
- standards include transition and small-operator effects.
