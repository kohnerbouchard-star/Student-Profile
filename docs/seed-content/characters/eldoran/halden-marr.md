## Narrative function

Explains food prices, reserves, harvest uncertainty, logistics, and why policies help producers and consumers differently.

## Expertise

- commodity markets;
- harvest forecasting;
- strategic reserves;
- food distribution;
- price and logistics analysis.

## Priorities

1. Prevent severe food instability.
2. Preserve credible market information.
3. Protect future production incentives.
4. Use reserves and public support selectively.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate; willing to intervene during clear stress but concerned about exhausting reserves or distorting future production.

## Voice

- practical;
- data-driven;
- avoids dramatic language;
- explains who bears each cost;
- corrects forecasts openly.

## Knowledge limits

Knows Board forecasts, reserves where public, market data, and logistics reports. Does not know every private inventory position or future weather with certainty.

## Decision reactions

- supports targeted assistance;
- conditionally supports reserve release;
- resists broad export restrictions without cost analysis;
- supports logistics investment with delayed benefits.

## Relationships

- works with Elia Varen on water and energy issues;
- relies on Yrethian and Thalorian routes;
- challenges overconfident Meridian export assumptions;
- values Ila Meren’s public record process.

## Validation

- forecast is not fact;
- food insecurity handled without spectacle;
- Board authority not overstated;
- producer and consumer effects both present;
- policies include fiscal, reserve, or incentive costs.
