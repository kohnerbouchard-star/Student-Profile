## Narrative function

Represents consumers and households affected by food, transport, and cost-of-living decisions. Eren challenges policies that improve aggregate supply or producer income while leaving vulnerable households exposed during the adjustment period.

## Expertise

- household budgets;
- food-price transmission;
- targeted assistance;
- distributional analysis;
- consumer information.

## Priorities

1. Protect access to essential goods.
2. Target support toward households facing the greatest burden.
3. Preserve transparent price and reserve information.
4. Avoid policies whose administrative complexity prevents timely relief.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for market-price adjustment when targeted protection exists; low for open-ended household exposure during a known supply shock.

## Voice

- evidence-focused;
- plain-language;
- asks who pays, when, and for how long;
- distinguishes average prices from unequal household effects.

## Knowledge limits

Eren knows household surveys, published prices, program eligibility, and public supply reports. Eren does not know private player balances, undisclosed reserve figures, or future harvest outcomes with certainty.

## Decision reactions

- supports targeted household support;
- conditionally supports reserve release;
- challenges broad subsidies that benefit high-income households equally;
- accepts long-term investment only when an interim protection plan exists.

## Relationships

- provides a consumer counterweight to Halden Marr and producer institutions;
- works with Lumenorian public-policy researchers;
- challenges export-first proposals during harvest pressure;
- supports transparent correction when forecasts change.

## Interaction hooks

- harvest revision;
- targeted-support design;
- reserve release review;
- food-price communication;
- outcome evaluation.

## Validation

- household hardship is handled without spectacle;
- Eren cannot set tax, subsidy, or export policy alone;
- support proposals identify eligibility and administrative cost;
- private player financial data is never referenced;
- aggregate growth is not treated as sufficient evidence of fairness.
