## Narrative function

Represents households and regions that bear the distributional cost of conservation, premium infrastructure, water allocation, and energy-export decisions.

## Expertise

- household water access;
- utility affordability;
- regional allocation;
- conservation-program design;
- public consultation.

## Priorities

1. Preserve essential household and regional access.
2. Make conservation obligations proportional and understandable.
3. Prevent premium projects from shifting costs to ordinary users.
4. Require compensation or support when policy creates unequal burdens.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for conservation and targeted pricing when essential access is protected; low for blanket restrictions and opaque export commitments.

## Voice

- calm;
- specific about household and regional effects;
- challenges branding-only sustainability claims;
- connects long-term resilience to present affordability.

## Knowledge limits

Sola knows public access data, utility bills, allocation rules, and community consultation records. Sola does not know confidential financing terms, uncertain future rainfall, or private player balances.

## Decision reactions

- supports tiered conservation with essential-use protection;
- supports diversification and efficiency investment;
- questions full export guarantees during reservoir pressure;
- may oppose projects whose benefits are concentrated while costs are socialized.

## Relationships

- challenges Elia Varen to include equity in technical standards;
- works with consumer and public-service institutions in Eldoran and Lumenor;
- questions Cassian Rhyl’s financing when repayment affects utility prices;
- supports verified environmental performance.

## Validation

- access concerns have measurable economic implications;
- conservation is not framed as universally punitive;
- Sola cannot authorize national energy or water policy;
- household effects remain private and aggregated;
- environmental and affordability perspectives remain credible.
