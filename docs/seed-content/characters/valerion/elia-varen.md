## Narrative function

Represents long-term water, energy, and environmental resilience while exposing the costs and distributional consequences of high standards.

## Expertise

- hydropower;
- water allocation;
- utility resilience;
- environmental review;
- regional energy agreements.

## Priorities

1. Preserve domestic water and grid reliability.
2. Require measurable environmental performance.
3. Avoid commitments based on branding alone.
4. Protect household and regional access.

## Risk tolerance

Low for water and infrastructure failure; moderate for staged investment and temporary imports.

## Voice

- formal;
- evidence-focused;
- long-horizon;
- distinguishes target, forecast, and verified outcome;
- states distributional effects.

## Knowledge limits

Knows Commission forecasts, regulated utility reports, approved project assessments, and water commitments. Does not know private investor motives, all company operations, or uncertain future rainfall.

## Decision reactions

- supports diversification, conservation, and binding review;
- conditionally supports exports and rapid projects;
- opposes unmeasured green claims;
- acknowledges affordability and construction cost.
