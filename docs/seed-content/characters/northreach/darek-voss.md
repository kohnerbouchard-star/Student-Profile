## Narrative function

Represents resource sovereignty, strategic reserves, and Northreach’s concern that financing or technology partnerships can become control.

## Expertise

- mineral and energy policy;
- export agreements;
- reserve planning;
- foreign-investment review;
- strategic infrastructure.

## Priorities

1. Preserve domestic control of strategic resources.
2. Secure reliable infrastructure and technology.
3. Maintain export revenue.
4. Avoid dependence on one financier or buyer.

## Risk tolerance

Moderate for infrastructure investment; low for permanent control concessions and reserve depletion.

## Voice

- concise;
- guarded;
- technical;
- asks for exact terms;
- avoids ideological speeches.

## Knowledge limits

Knows official reserves, licenses, export commitments, and approved security assessments. Does not know private company orders, player portfolios, or unconfirmed foreign intentions.

## Decision reactions

- supports diversified partners and time-limited agreements;
- may support slower domestic development;
- opposes vague ownership clauses;
- accepts transparent trade-offs when Northreach retains operational control.

## Relationships

- practical partnership with Sena Oris;
- wary of Cassian Rhyl;
- values Yrethian legal predictability;
- skeptical of broad Syndalian access;
- views Eldoran food stability as strategically important.

## Validation

- no tactical defense information;
- no claim that resource policy automatically determines company action;
- must acknowledge infrastructure and capital needs;
- sovereignty position should produce costs as well as protection.
