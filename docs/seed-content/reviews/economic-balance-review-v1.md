# Economic and Balance Review v1

Review status: changes required
Scope reviewed: foundation frameworks, ten country files, Meridian arc, five contracts, seven events
Review perspective: economic systems and incentive integrity

## Executive finding

The foundation is economically coherent enough to continue concept development, but it is not yet ready for numeric seeding. The strongest aspect is that each country’s advantage has a corresponding vulnerability and each event specifies directional effects, duration, and recovery. The main blockers are unresolved currency treatment, absence of a unified reward-and-price model, lack of final market entities, and no quantitative viability simulation.

## Strengths

### Country differentiation

All ten countries have distinct value creation:

- resources;
- trade and insurance;
- flexible logistics;
- research;
- food and distribution;
- clean energy and services;
- institutions and education;
- finance;
- industrial production;
- data and security.

The files generally avoid giving a country simultaneous dominance in capital, resources, stability, technology, and trade.

### Strength-vulnerability symmetry

Examples:

- Northreach gains strategic value from resources but becomes exposed to concentration and ownership disputes.
- Yrethia gains value from rules and insurance but becomes vulnerable to capacity and loss events.
- Solvend gains high-value growth but becomes rate and talent sensitive.
- Xalvoria gains speed through capital but accumulates correlated credit and legitimacy risk.
- Syndalis gains demand during cyber risk but may suffer larger trust losses.

This is an appropriate production principle.

### Event economics

The pilot events:

- distinguish forecasts from confirmed outcomes;
- use semantic effect classes instead of invented numbers;
- identify winners and losers;
- include delayed effects;
- define recovery;
- avoid automatic global market movement.

### Contract rewards

The contract files correctly defer final monetary values and include duplicate-payout safeguards.

## Blocking findings

### E-01: Official role of ECO unresolved

Severity: blocking

The official currency manifest contains ten country currencies and no ECO record. Existing product behavior may still use ECO as a classroom or fallback unit.

Required decision:

Classify ECO as exactly one of:

- classroom reward unit;
- settlement unit;
- accounting reference unit;
- explicit fallback;
- deprecated legacy term.

Until resolved, no final contract reward, store price, attendance payout, or bank product should be approved.

### E-02: Exchange-index methodology unresolved

Severity: blocking

Required specification:

- index direction;
- conversion formula;
- transaction-time versus current-time rate;
- rounding;
- precision;
- fallback behavior;
- treatment when index is unavailable;
- whether Admin-authored prices are stored in local currency, reference currency, or both.

### E-03: Unified player-economy model absent

Severity: blocking

Attendance, contracts, store prices, banking yield, fees, and progression rewards must be simulated together.

Required output:

- routine income baseline;
- substantial contract baseline;
- expected weekly or session income;
- price bands and target acquisition time;
- maximum sustainable interest yield;
- borrowing affordability;
- sink strength;
- inflation and conversion treatment.

### E-04: No quantitative country viability test

Severity: blocking before final starting values

Required scenarios for every country:

- stable baseline;
- one country-specific negative event;
- high interest rate;
- trade disruption;
- weak global growth;
- recovery period.

Measure:

- ability to earn;
- market opportunity;
- contract availability;
- price access;
- recovery time;
- relative disadvantage.

### E-05: Market universe not yet instantiated

Severity: blocking for market balance

The framework proposes 30 companies but no production-shaped company files exist.

Required before market approval:

- 3 companies per country;
- financial arithmetic;
- unique tickers;
- company-specific event exposure;
- national index rules;
- portfolio fixtures.

### E-06: Simultaneous warning limit not finalized

Severity: major

The arc states that the standard profile should not activate all warnings. Final policy is required.

Recommendation:

- standard classroom: maximum 2 active capacity warnings before the security event;
- policy-intensive: maximum 3;
- crisis-response: 3–4 with explicit instructor selection;
- sandbox: instructor controlled.

### E-07: Severe event cumulative cap undefined

Severity: major

The security intrusion is a major event. A cumulative cap is needed for simultaneous major and severe effects to avoid compounding multiple crisis penalties.

### E-08: Reserve, debt, trust, water, labor, and capacity concepts lack authoritative numeric representation

Severity: major

These may remain narrative metadata, but the catalog must not imply mechanical change unless a supported indicator or rule exists.

## Nonblocking findings

### E-N01: Country currencies need player-facing economic identities

The country files have directional descriptions. Add an eventual currency profile file per currency with volatility class, display rules, and exposure.

### E-N02: Opportunity-cost language is strong but acquisition-time examples are absent

Add illustrative non-final scenarios to the balance workbook or report.

### E-N03: Policy costs need units

When numeric content begins, every fiscal, reserve, capacity, or time cost must have a defined unit.
