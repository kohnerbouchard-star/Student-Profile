# Gameplay and Classroom Learning Review v1

Review status: changes required
Scope reviewed: country concepts, Meridian arc, contracts, events, tutorials framework, progression framework
Review perspective: player agency, pacing, cognitive load, instructional value, and instructor burden

## Executive finding

The content has strong economics learning potential and avoids making players passive observers. The five-contract chain is coherent and the event structure supports evidence-based decision-making. The pilot is not classroom-ready because pacing, expected effort, rubric detail, instructor review burden, accessibility, and group-decision handling remain unfinished.

## Strengths

### G-S01: Learning objectives are connected to actual choices

The pilot covers:

- comparative advantage;
- infrastructure and public goods;
- debt and ownership;
- uncertainty;
- supply shocks;
- distributional effects;
- reflection and revision.

### G-S02: Contracts escalate logically

The chain moves from global understanding to country analysis, comparative policy, crisis response, and outcome reflection.

### G-S03: Players are not graded on one preferred ideology

Admin guidance consistently focuses on reasoning and evidence.

### G-S04: Failure and revision are supported

Contracts permit changes requested and avoid balance penalties for ordinary expiry.

### G-S05: Country assignment has meaningful differences

Country-specific analysis requires actual strengths and dependencies rather than name substitution.

## Blocking findings

### G-01: Contract effort and classroom timing undefined

Severity: blocking for classroom pilot

Each contract needs:

- expected student time;
- expected classroom or homework setting;
- suggested response length;
- due-window recommendation;
- prerequisite lesson or glossary;
- teacher review time estimate;
- fast-review rubric.

### G-02: Instructor review burden too high if all contracts are manual

Severity: blocking at classroom scale

Required design:

- identify which fields can be automatically checked;
- create a concise rubric;
- allow batch review where safe;
- use sample strong and weak responses;
- limit narrative contracts requiring individualized feedback;
- decide whether group contracts reduce or increase total review load.

### G-03: Cognitive-load cap needs final policy

Severity: major

Recommendation:

- no more than 2 active global or country crisis warnings in the standard profile;
- one primary action request at a time for introductory players;
- glossary and country summary visible without leaving the task;
- complex policy comparisons divided into structured fields.

### G-04: Group decision model absent

Severity: major

The Corridor resolution needs a classroom process:

- individual recommendations only;
- country-team recommendation;
- classroom vote;
- instructor synthesis;
- automatic aggregation.

The content must not assume a group voting engine exists.

### G-05: Accessibility and reading-level validation incomplete

Severity: major

Required:

- reading-level target;
- short summaries for every long briefing;
- keyboard and screen-reader path;
- non-map access to locations;
- no color-only event severity;
- plain-language glossary;
- reduced-motion consideration for event presentation.

### G-06: Reward and progression connection undefined

Severity: major

Contracts mention reward bands and achievement progress, but:

- amount;
- progression threshold;
- chain-completion bonus;
- repeatability;
- attendance interaction;
- late or revised submission treatment

are unresolved.

## Learning-design findings

### G-L01: Reflection contract is strong

Pass. It explicitly compares predicted and observed outcomes.

### G-L02: Security event must not require technical cyber knowledge

Pass at concept level. Keep response focused on governance, access, continuity, and uncertainty.

### G-L03: Country fairness needs instructional parity

Every country should have:

- equally meaningful evidence;
- at least one reasonable strategic path;
- equivalent contract complexity;
- no requirement for background knowledge unique to one assignment.

### G-L04: Players need visible cause-and-effect history

The UI or briefing must eventually show:

- what changed;
- what decision preceded it;
- which effect is immediate;
- which effect is delayed;
- what remains uncertain.

### G-L05: Tutorial sequence should precede first irreversible action

The first stock purchase, loan, item redemption, or major story decision should not be required before the player receives the relevant tutorial or confirmation.

## Instructor operations findings

Required Admin support or documented manual process:

- assign or release contract chain;
- view active arc and event;
- identify expiring work;
- review submissions quickly;
- see reward status separately from approval;
- cancel an event without orphaning accepted work;
- reset a fixture scenario;
- explain why a player sees a given interaction.

Unsupported Admin functions must remain documented as planned rather than assumed.
