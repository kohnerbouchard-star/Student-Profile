Stage: competing models
Implementation status: field mapping pending

## Player-facing summary

Analyze how the Meridian Corridor could help and harm your assigned country.

## Objective

Use the country profile to identify what the country supplies, what it needs from others, which Corridor model best fits its interests, and which risk requires protection.

## Required output

1. Country’s two most relevant strengths.
2. Two external dependencies.
3. One company or industry likely to benefit.
4. One company, industry, or public group that could bear a cost.
5. Preferred Corridor approach and reasoning.
6. One condition that should be added before the country participates.

## Evidence requirements

- assigned-country profile;
- one other country profile;
- one institution record;
- one active or proposed event, where available.

## Learning objectives

- comparative advantage;
- specialization;
- dependency;
- distributional effects;
- negotiation from a defined economic position.

## Eligibility

- approved completion of `contract.meridian.evaluate-corridor.v1` or instructor override;
- assigned country available;
- Meridian arc at competing-models stage or later.

## Country-specific variation

The prompt should inject the correct country strengths, dependencies, and active warnings. It must not rely on ten separate definitions that only change the country name.

## Completion rubric

Approved when:

- the player uses real characteristics of the assigned country;
- at least one trade-off affects a group or sector differently;
- the preferred model is supported by evidence;
- the proposed condition is within country or institutional authority.

Changes requested when:

- content is generic;
- another country’s features are incorrectly attributed;
- recommendation ignores a stated dependency;
- proposed condition requires unsupported or unlimited authority.

## Reward policy

Reward band: standard analysis.

Final monetary value: pending combined-economy review.

Narrative outcome:

- records country-analysis completion;
- unlocks financing and governance comparison;
- may contribute to country reputation or achievement progress if supported.

## Narrative effects

This contract does not directly commit the country. It records a player recommendation and may affect later interaction framing when the runtime supports player-choice memory.

## Failure and expiry

Revision allowed during the active stage.

If country assignment changes before approval, the submission must either remain tied to the original assignment with clear audit history or be cancelled without payout. Silent reassignment is not allowed.

## Admin guidance

Do not grade players on selecting the instructor’s preferred Corridor model. Evaluate country-specific reasoning, evidence, and awareness of costs.

## Validation

- assigned country derived from authenticated session state;
- browser cannot submit an ownership UUID;
- country profile reference valid;
- no duplicate approval or reward;
- assignment change behavior explicit;
- contract remains useful under multiple scenario warnings.
