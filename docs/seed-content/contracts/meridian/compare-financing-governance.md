# Compare Meridian Financing and Governance

Version: 1.0.0-draft
Maturity: draft
Family: finance and policy analysis

Implementation status: field mapping pending

## Player-facing summary

Compare the four leading Corridor models and recommend a structure that balances funding, speed, ownership, legitimacy, and resilience.

## Models

- Xalvorian finance-first;
- Lumenorian multilateral governance;
- Yrethian trade-and-logistics;
- Dravenlok–Northreach industrial-security.

## Required output

Complete a comparison containing:

1. likely source of funding;
2. decision authority;
3. primary benefit;
4. primary economic cost;
5. primary political or institutional risk;
6. country likely to benefit most;
7. country or group likely to require protection;
8. recommended hybrid or selected model;
9. two safeguards.

## Learning objectives

- debt and equity;
- public infrastructure;
- governance and principal-agent problems;
- opportunity cost of delay;
- concentration and diversification;
- legitimacy and transaction costs.

## Eligibility

- country-exposure contract approved or instructor override;
- all four model briefings available;
- no terminal Corridor resolution.

## Completion rubric

Approved when:

- all four models are compared fairly;
- funding and governance are treated as separate but connected decisions;
- recommendation includes costs;
- safeguards respond to identified risks;
- no model is described as costless.

Changes requested when:

- recommendation is unsupported;
- financing terms are confused with currency conversion or player banking;
- one model is caricatured;
- safeguards require powers no institution has.

## Reward policy

Reward band: substantial analysis.

Final value: pending economy review.

Potential nonmonetary result:

- unlock crisis-response contract;
- progression toward negotiation or policy achievement;
- institution-specific follow-up interaction.

## Narrative effects

The recommendation may contribute to session-level support or interaction variation only if a supported aggregation model exists. Otherwise it remains a player analysis record and does not silently change the world.

## Failure and expiry

Revision allowed until the security crisis or governance resolution closes the comparison stage.

If the arc advances, an accepted contract should remain completable under a documented grace policy or be cancelled transparently without duplicate reward.

## Admin guidance

Focus on trade-off reasoning. A hybrid model is not automatically superior; it must explain added complexity and responsibility.

## Validation

- model records exist;
- no unsupported collective voting implied;
- reward issuance idempotent;
- grace behavior defined;
- financing language does not conflate sovereign, company, and player debt;
- recommendation remains auditable.
