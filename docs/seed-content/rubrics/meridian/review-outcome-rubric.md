# Review the Meridian Outcome — Review Rubric

Applies to: `contract.meridian.review-outcome.v1`
Version: 1.0.0-draft
Target student time: 20 minutes
Target review time: 90–120 seconds
Suggested response length: 220–350 words or equivalent structured fields

## Fast approval rubric

Score each category 0, 1, or 2.

### 1. Outcome accuracy

- 0: describes an outcome that did not occur.
- 1: names the correct outcome with limited supporting evidence.
- 2: identifies the actual resolution and at least one decisive recorded condition.

### 2. Immediate effects

- 0: no immediate result or only one-sided praise or criticism.
- 1: identifies a positive result or cost.
- 2: identifies both one immediate benefit and one immediate cost or lost opportunity.

### 3. Delayed and distributional effects

- 0: absent or generic.
- 1: identifies a delayed effect or a beneficiary/cost bearer.
- 2: identifies one delayed consequence, one beneficiary, and one party bearing additional risk or cost.

### 4. Comparison with prior recommendation

- 0: rewrites or ignores the earlier position.
- 1: notes agreement or disagreement without evidence.
- 2: compares expected and observed outcomes and identifies what the earlier recommendation captured or missed.

### 5. Revision quality

- 0: no revision or unrelated proposal.
- 1: relevant revision with limited implementation detail.
- 2: proposes one feasible change tied to an observed weakness, responsible institution, and review condition.

## Decision guidance

- 9–10: approve and complete chain.
- 7–8: approve when all required output fields are present.
- 5–6: focused revision.
- 0–4: incomplete or inconsistent with session outcome.

## Automatic checks suitable for tooling

- authoritative resolution ID or runtime outcome reference present;
- prior Meridian submission reference exists when required;
- immediate benefit and cost fields present;
- delayed effect present;
- beneficiary and cost bearer present;
- revision present;
- chain completion not already issued;
- reward not already issued.

## Strong response indicators

- uses actual recorded conditions rather than generic model descriptions;
- distinguishes a successful launch from equitable benefit;
- explains why an accepted safeguard did or did not work;
- identifies an unintended consequence;
- revises a prior recommendation without pretending earlier uncertainty did not exist;
- proposes a follow-up that the named institution can undertake.

## Weak response indicators

- judges the outcome only by stock gains or total growth;
- says the original answer was “right” without comparison;
- claims every country benefited equally;
- identifies a delayed effect that had not occurred or been scheduled;
- proposes reversing completed transactions or history without a rollback process.

## Changes-requested templates

### Wrong resolution

“Use the actual session resolution and the recorded decisive conditions. Revise references to a model that was proposed but not selected.”

### Missing distributional analysis

“Identify one beneficiary and one country, company, institution, or group bearing additional cost or risk.”

### Prior recommendation not compared

“Compare what you expected with what occurred. Identify one part your earlier recommendation anticipated and one part it did not.”

### Revision lacks implementation

“Name the institution responsible for the revision and define the condition used to review whether it worked.”

## Sample strong structure

1. Actual resolution.
2. Decisive condition.
3. Immediate benefit.
4. Immediate cost.
5. Delayed consequence.
6. Beneficiary.
7. Cost or risk bearer.
8. Comparison with prior recommendation.
9. Follow-up revision.
10. Responsible institution and review trigger.

## Reviewer safeguards

- do not require the student to reject their earlier view;
- reward evidence-based revision, not conformity;
- do not expose another player’s private submission;
- use authoritative session outcome;
- apply chain completion and reward exactly once;
- preserve original and revised submissions in audit history.
