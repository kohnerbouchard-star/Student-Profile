# Analyze Your Country’s Meridian Exposure — Review Rubric

Applies to: `contract.meridian.analyze-country-exposure.v1`
Version: 1.0.0-draft
Target student time: 20–25 minutes
Target review time: 90–120 seconds
Suggested response length: 220–350 words or equivalent structured fields

## Fast approval rubric

Score each category 0, 1, or 2.

### 1. Country accuracy

- 0: uses incorrect or generic country characteristics.
- 1: identifies valid characteristics but does not connect them to the Corridor.
- 2: accurately uses at least two strengths and two external dependencies from the assigned-country profile.

### 2. Distributional analysis

- 0: says only that the country wins or loses.
- 1: identifies one affected industry or group.
- 2: identifies at least one likely beneficiary and one party bearing additional cost or risk.

### 3. Strategic recommendation

- 0: no model or recommendation selected.
- 1: recommendation stated with limited support.
- 2: recommendation follows from country characteristics and includes at least one meaningful cost.

### 4. Participation condition

- 0: condition is vague or outside any institution’s authority.
- 1: condition is relevant but incomplete.
- 2: condition directly mitigates an identified country vulnerability and names the institution or process responsible.

### 5. Comparative evidence

- 0: no other country or institution used.
- 1: comparison named but not analyzed.
- 2: explains one dependency, partnership, or conflict involving another country and one institution.

## Decision guidance

- 9–10: approve.
- 7–8: approve if all required output fields are present.
- 5–6: focused revision.
- 0–4: incomplete or country-generic.

## Automatic checks suitable for tooling

- assigned country derived from authenticated session;
- all required sections present;
- at least two country-strength references;
- at least two dependency references;
- one other official country code referenced;
- one institution referenced;
- one participation condition supplied;
- no duplicate approved completion.

## Strong response indicators

- Northreach response considers both mineral leverage and food/technology dependence.
- Yrethia response considers throughput and insurance risk.
- Thaloris response distinguishes flexibility from legitimacy.
- Solvend response distinguishes research ownership from funding.
- Eldoran response separates producer and consumer effects.
- Valerion response separates environmental credibility from affordability.
- Lumenor response identifies legitimacy and delay.
- Xalvoria response identifies finance speed and concentration.
- Dravenlok response considers employment, energy, and components.
- Syndalis response considers security effectiveness and trust.

## Weak response indicators

- same answer would fit every country;
- claims country-specific powers not present in the profile;
- assumes the country is self-sufficient;
- names only benefits;
- treats a working event forecast as a confirmed permanent condition.

## Changes-requested templates

### Generic analysis

“This answer could describe several countries. Revise it using two strengths, two dependencies, and one institution from your assigned-country profile.”

### Missing distributional effect

“Identify who benefits and who bears the cost inside or outside your country. National growth does not affect every group equally.”

### Unsupported condition

“Revise the participation condition so an identified institution could realistically implement or negotiate it.”

## Sample strong structure

1. Country advantage.
2. Second advantage.
3. External dependencies.
4. Likely beneficiary.
5. Likely cost bearer.
6. Preferred model.
7. Cost of that model.
8. Required safeguard.
9. Partner or counterpart country.

## Reviewer safeguards

- verify assigned country before review;
- do not accept country-name substitution as customization;
- do not reveal other players’ private submissions;
- if country assignment changed, apply the documented cancellation or original-assignment policy;
- reward only once after authoritative approval.
