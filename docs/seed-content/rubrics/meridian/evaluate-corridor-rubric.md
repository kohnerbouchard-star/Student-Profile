# Evaluate the Meridian Corridor — Review Rubric

Applies to: `contract.meridian.evaluate-corridor.v1`
Version: 1.0.0-draft
Target student time: 15–20 minutes
Target review time: 60–90 seconds
Suggested response length: 180–300 words or equivalent structured fields

## Fast approval rubric

Score each category 0, 1, or 2.

### 1. Benefits and risks

- 0: only benefits or only risks; statements are generic.
- 1: includes both, but one side lacks Corridor-specific reasoning.
- 2: identifies at least three plausible benefits and three material risks tied to infrastructure, ownership, capacity, data, trade, energy, finance, or governance.

### 2. Stakeholder coverage

- 0: names fewer than two relevant countries or institutions.
- 1: names several entities without explaining their interests.
- 2: explains the role or interest of at least four relevant countries or institutions.

### 3. Trade-off reasoning

- 0: no trade-off or treats the project as costless.
- 1: identifies one trade-off without distinguishing time horizon.
- 2: identifies one short-term and one long-term trade-off and explains who bears the cost.

### 4. Evidence and uncertainty

- 0: contradicts the briefing or states forecasts as facts.
- 1: uses some evidence but leaves uncertainty unclear.
- 2: uses required sources and asks one actionable unresolved question.

## Decision guidance

- 7–8: approve.
- 5–6: approve when all required fields are present; otherwise request a focused revision.
- 3–4: changes requested.
- 0–2: incomplete or off-task.

A low score is not an ideological disagreement. Review reasoning and evidence only.

## Automatic checks suitable for tooling

- required sections present;
- minimum number of benefit, risk, and stakeholder entries;
- at least one country and one institution reference;
- response length within configured bounds or approved alternative format;
- no empty required field;
- no duplicate approved completion.

Automatic checks must not decide whether economic reasoning is correct without a separately reviewed evaluation system.

## Strong response indicators

- recognizes that lower trade cost can coexist with debt and ownership risk;
- distinguishes total growth from distribution of gains;
- identifies a country-specific dependency;
- treats the security or capacity issue as unresolved rather than certain;
- asks for a decision-relevant missing fact.

## Weak response indicators

- “The Corridor is good because trade is good.”
- lists country names without explaining their roles;
- treats Xalvoria, Syndalis, Thaloris, or another country as inherently bad;
- claims construction has already started;
- proposes unlimited funding or resources.

## Changes-requested templates

### One-sided analysis

“You identified several benefits, but the assessment also requires material costs and risks. Add at least three Corridor-specific risks and explain who would bear them.”

### Missing evidence

“Use at least two country profiles and one institution proposal or warning. Revise the response so the recommendation is grounded in the current briefings.”

### Forecast treated as fact

“Separate confirmed conditions from forecasts. Identify what is known now and what remains uncertain.”

## Sample strong outline

- Benefit: lower logistics cost through connected rail and ports.
- Benefit: improved resilience through alternate routes and shared data.
- Benefit: new demand for construction, research, and energy systems.
- Risk: concentrated financing may transfer influence to the lead creditor.
- Risk: centralized data access may improve security while creating trust and privacy concerns.
- Risk: higher exports may pressure domestic food, energy, or strategic reserves.
- Stakeholders: Lumenor, Xalvoria, Yrethia, Northreach, Syndalis.
- Short-term trade-off: faster construction versus reduced review.
- Long-term trade-off: integrated infrastructure versus dependency.
- Unresolved question: what ownership or access rights continue after repayment or emergency expiry?

## Reviewer safeguards

- do not require a preferred model;
- do not reward length beyond evidence quality;
- do not penalize cautious recommendations;
- preserve revision history;
- issue reward only after authoritative approval succeeds.
