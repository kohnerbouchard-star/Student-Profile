# Compare Meridian Financing and Governance — Review Rubric

Applies to: `contract.meridian.compare-financing-governance.v1`
Version: 1.0.0-draft
Target student time: 20–25 minutes plus 10–12 minutes of model review
Target review time: 2–3 minutes
Required format: structured comparison matrix plus 200–300 word recommendation

## Matrix completion check

For each of the four models, the student must identify:

- funding source;
- decision authority;
- primary benefit;
- economic cost;
- institutional or political risk;
- likely beneficiary;
- required safeguard.

Missing one isolated cell may receive a focused revision. A mostly incomplete model row is not approval-ready.

## Analytic rubric

Score each category 0, 1, or 2.

### 1. Fair model comparison

- 0: caricatures or ignores one or more models.
- 1: describes all models but analyzes some unevenly.
- 2: gives every model at least one real advantage, one cost, and one risk.

### 2. Financing accuracy

- 0: confuses sovereign project finance with player banking, currency conversion, or free funding.
- 1: identifies debt or ownership but omits repayment, return, or risk.
- 2: distinguishes debt, equity, shared funding, guarantees, and the consequences of delay.

### 3. Governance accuracy

- 0: treats governance as the same as financing.
- 1: identifies decision authority without explaining accountability.
- 2: explains who decides, who can constrain the decision maker, and how disputes or review occur.

### 4. Recommendation quality

- 0: selects a model without evidence.
- 1: recommendation uses some evidence but ignores a major cost.
- 2: recommendation follows from country and Corridor conditions and explicitly accepts at least one cost.

### 5. Safeguards and implementation

- 0: safeguards are vague or impossible.
- 1: safeguards address risk but omit authority, expiry, review, or enforcement.
- 2: proposes two realistic safeguards with responsible institutions and implementation conditions.

## Decision guidance

- 9–10: approve.
- 7–8: approve if the matrix is complete and no major economic misconception remains.
- 5–6: changes requested.
- 0–4: incomplete or conceptually inaccurate.

## Automatic checks suitable for tooling

- four model rows present;
- required columns present;
- recommendation present;
- at least two safeguards present;
- at least one country or institution cited in the recommendation;
- response submitted before the configured stage closes;
- duplicate approved completion blocked.

## Strong response indicators

- identifies the opportunity cost of slow multilateral review;
- identifies the concentration and dependency cost of finance-first execution;
- recognizes that trade-led development may distribute benefits unevenly;
- recognizes industrial guarantees can create employment and lock-in;
- explains why a hybrid adds coordination cost rather than assuming it is automatically best;
- distinguishes advisory oversight from binding control.

## Weak response indicators

- “Use every model together” without defining authority or cost;
- “Xalvoria pays for everything” without repayment or ownership;
- “Lumenor makes it fair” without decision rules;
- “Yrethia runs trade” without capacity or insurance;
- “Dravenlok builds it” without component, energy, or demand risk.

## Changes-requested templates

### Financing and governance conflated

“Revise the comparison so funding and decision authority are separate. Identify who supplies capital, who makes operating decisions, and who can review or constrain those decisions.”

### Hybrid without cost

“Your recommendation combines several models, but it does not explain the additional coordination, accountability, or timing cost. Add those costs and identify the final decision authority.”

### Safeguard lacks owner

“Name the institution responsible for applying or reviewing each safeguard and define when the safeguard expires or is reconsidered.”

## Sample strong recommendation pattern

- Select a primary governance structure.
- Select one financing structure.
- Explain why the assigned country accepts them.
- State one short-term cost.
- State one long-term concentration or fragmentation risk.
- Add one financial safeguard.
- Add one environmental, labor, compliance, or data safeguard.
- Identify the authority responsible for each.

## Reviewer safeguards

- do not reward complexity by itself;
- do not require a hybrid recommendation;
- do not treat concentrated or multilateral structures as inherently ethical or unethical;
- preserve the recommendation as evidence rather than automatically changing world state;
- issue reward once after authoritative approval.
