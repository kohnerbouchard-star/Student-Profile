## Purpose

Create a resource-sovereignty and supply-risk decision around Meridian mineral and energy commitments.

## Trigger requirements

- Meridian arc active;
- strategic-resource demand or commitment elevated;
- no existing full export ban;
- simultaneous-warning limit respected.

## Player-facing report

Northreach has opened a review of proposed long-term mineral and energy commitments. Existing shipments continue, but new Meridian volumes may be delayed until reserve, ownership, and infrastructure conditions are clarified.

## Fact status

Confirmed policy review. Future restrictions remain uncertain.

## Affected entities

- Northreach resource and logistics companies;
- Solvend advanced manufacturing;
- Dravenlok industry;
- Meridian construction and technology schedule;
- Northreach Credit outlook.

## Immediate effects

Semantic directions:

- strategic mineral availability: minor to moderate negative;
- spot or reference price pressure: mild positive;
- Northreach producer outlook: mixed;
- Solvend and Dravenlok input risk: moderate negative;
- Corridor schedule confidence: minor negative.

## Decision options

1. Approve a long-term supply agreement with ownership safeguards.
2. Reduce volumes and preserve reserves.
3. require domestic processing before export.
4. diversify buyers and financing partners.
5. delay commitments pending infrastructure upgrades.

## Delayed effects

- long-term agreement improves certainty but limits flexibility;
- reserve protection reduces current revenue;
- domestic processing requires capital and time;
- diversified partners reduce concentration but increase complexity;
- delay may encourage substitutes and alternate suppliers.

## Recovery

- terms agreed;
- infrastructure improved;
- supply volumes revised;
- substitute input adopted;
- joint Solvend–Northreach technology agreement.

## Contract and interaction hooks

- Darek Voss mineral-access interaction;
- resource-sovereignty analysis;
- Solvend partnership negotiation;
- disruption-response contract.

## Failure and expiry

The review resolves through approval, conditional approval, reduced commitment, or withdrawal. It should not remain indefinitely active without a follow-up review date.

## Validation

- review is not described as an embargo;
- existing versus new commitments distinguished;
- resource effects bounded;
- producers do not automatically benefit from every restriction;
- domestic processing requires cost and time;
- event session scoped.
