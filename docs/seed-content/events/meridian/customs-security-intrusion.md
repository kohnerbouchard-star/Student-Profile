## Purpose

Create the pilot’s primary crisis: cargo and payment records become unreliable, forcing decisions about security access, trade continuity, privacy, insurance, and international control.

## Trigger requirements

- Meridian arc active after competing models are introduced;
- participating systems or route definitions exist in narrative state;
- no active unresolved equivalent security crisis;
- scenario profile permits major event;
- instructor or approved schedule may activate.

Threshold-based activation should not be implemented until authoritative trigger data exists.

## Player-facing report

Several Meridian customs and payment-verification systems are reporting conflicting records. Some cargo cannot be matched confidently to payment and inspection data.

No source has been confirmed. Authorities are investigating whether the disruption results from malicious access, software failure, corrupted synchronization, or more than one cause.

## Fact status

Confirmed operational discrepancy. Cause and attribution unresolved.

## Affected entities

Primary:

- Syndalis cyber and network institutions;
- Yrethian customs and insurers;
- Thalorian routes and verification;
- Meridian trade and finance operations.

Secondary:

- companies dependent on time-sensitive cargo;
- Xalvorian finance and payment systems;
- Solvend technical providers;
- global confidence.

## Immediate effects

Semantic directions:

- verified throughput: moderate to major negative;
- insurance risk: moderate positive pressure;
- manual processing cost: moderate positive;
- cyber-defense demand: moderate positive;
- fintech and platform trust: moderate negative;
- broad market confidence: minor to moderate negative.

Cybersecurity demand must not guarantee positive net performance for every Syndalian company; incident liability and trust losses may dominate for affected firms.

## Decision options

1. Grant broad temporary Syndalian access with expiry and independent audit.
2. Use a federated response controlled by national systems.
3. Limit Syndalis to technical assistance while Lumenor coordinates oversight.
4. Move critical cargo to manual verification and lower throughput.
5. Suspend selected Corridor operations until integrity is restored.

Availability may vary by technical support. Unsupported options should remain recommendations rather than direct runtime commands.

## Immediate consequences

### Broad temporary access

- faster unified visibility;
- concentration and privacy concern;
- trust depends on expiry and audit.

### Federated response

- preserves national control;
- slower coordination;
- inconsistent data-sharing risk.

### Assistance with independent oversight

- balances trust and access;
- increases process and staffing cost;
- may delay containment.

### Manual verification

- reduces digital exposure;
- sharply reduces throughput;
- increases labor and error risk.

### Limited suspension

- protects integrity;
- delays cargo and finance;
- may increase confidence later if investigation succeeds.

## Delayed effects

- successful audited response improves resilience and standards;
- overbroad access may trigger trust and privacy backlash;
- fragmented response may produce prolonged incompatibility;
- manual fallback may create congestion;
- unresolved attribution may affect diplomacy and company trust.

## Recovery

Recovery requires:

- record integrity restored;
- pending cargo reconciled or rejected through an authoritative process;
- payment status verified;
- incident scope documented;
- access revoked or normalized according to decision;
- independent or authorized review completed;
- correction issued if attribution changes.
