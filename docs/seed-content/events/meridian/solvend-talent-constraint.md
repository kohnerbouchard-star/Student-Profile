## Purpose

Show how human capital can constrain advanced projects even when funding, research, and demand are strong.

## Trigger requirements

- Meridian technical work active;
- Solvend research or company demand elevated;
- no resolved talent-surplus event;
- simultaneous-warning limit respected.

## Player-facing report

Solvend’s Advanced Systems Consortium reports that specialized engineering vacancies are delaying validation and deployment work. The problem is concentrated in selected roles rather than a general labor shortage.

## Fact status

Confirmed staffing and project data; future delays remain forecast.

## Affected entities

- Solvend technology and aerospace companies;
- Meridian systems schedule;
- education and training institutions;
- foreign recruitment and partnership decisions;
- Solvend Volt outlook.

## Immediate effects

Semantic directions:

- Solvend project capacity: moderate negative;
- skilled wages and retention cost: mild positive pressure;
- training and education demand: mild positive;
- growth-company outlook: mixed;
- Corridor technical schedule: minor to moderate negative.

## Decision options

1. increase retention and compensation.
2. accelerate training and apprenticeships.
3. recruit internationally.
4. outsource selected work.
5. delay or narrow the project.

## Delayed effects

- higher compensation increases cost but supports retention;
- training is slower but builds domestic capacity;
- recruitment may improve capacity and create housing or integration pressure;
- outsourcing speeds delivery but transfers knowledge;
- delay preserves quality but reduces near-term revenue.

## Recovery

- successful retention;
- training graduates;
- international recruitment;
- project reprioritization;
- technology simplification;
- shared work with Northreach, Lumenor, or Syndalis.

## Contract and interaction hooks

- Sena Oris capacity warning;
- talent-retention policy contract;
- project-scope decision;
- disruption-response contract.

## Failure and expiry

The event resolves when capacity recovers or the project is formally reduced. It should not repeatedly penalize Solvend without new triggers.

## Validation

- shortage is role-specific;
- workers are not treated as interchangeable units;
- wage, training, immigration, and outsourcing options all have costs;
- no real immigration stereotype or political copy;
- effects bounded and session scoped.
