## Purpose

Provide independent public-interest reporting, verification, corrections, and accessible explanation of economic and institutional events.

## Mandate

- verify public claims;
- publish clearly labeled fact, forecast, allegation, and analysis;
- issue visible corrections;
- request public records;
- explain complex economic decisions;
- preserve links between original and corrected reports.

## Authority limits

The Trust cannot determine legal guilt, direct policy, disclose confidential player submissions, or claim access to classified evidence it has not received.

## Economic role

Reduces information asymmetry and rumor-driven transaction costs while accepting that verification and correction require time and institutional independence.

## Core tension

Timely public information versus evidence quality, source protection, confidentiality, and the risk of premature certainty.

## Contract and event hooks

- Forum announcement;
- forecast correction;
- security-attribution dispute;
- public-trust crisis;
- resolution explanation.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through accurate labeling, visible correction, independent evidence, and consistent standards. Trust falls through sensationalism, concealed correction, political favoritism, or misleading claims of certainty.

## Validation

- news remains distinct from institution press releases;
- corrections preserve history;
- no private player or credential data;
- fact status is explicit;
- Trust authority remains journalistic, not judicial or executive.
