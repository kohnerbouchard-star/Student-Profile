## Purpose

Provide a credible multilateral venue for the Meridian Corridor and later international negotiations.

## Public mandate

- convene participating countries;
- publish draft charters and agendas;
- organize technical and policy committees;
- record agreements and reservations;
- facilitate arbitration and follow-up reviews;
- provide neutral public communication.

## Authority

The Forum may:

- issue analysis and negotiation contracts;
- request country submissions;
- publish nonbinding proposals;
- certify that a procedural stage was completed;
- coordinate shared briefings.

The Forum may not:

- spend national funds without authorization;
- force a country to participate;
- own Corridor assets by default;
- impose sanctions;
- independently apply economic policy.

## Economic role

The Forum lowers transaction costs by creating shared rules, trusted records, and a repeatable negotiation process. Its value increases when countries disagree but still prefer cooperation to fragmentation.

## Primary tensions

- legitimacy versus speed;
- equal representation versus contribution-based influence;
- transparency versus confidential negotiation;
- procedural consistency versus emergency flexibility.

## Narrative role

Lead institution for `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`.

It introduces the arc, presents competing models, records country positions, communicates resolution, and initiates follow-up review.

## Lead character

`character.lumenor.ila-meren.v1`

## Contract authority

May issue:

- Corridor evaluation;
- country exposure analysis;
- governance comparison;
- multilateral negotiation preparation;
- outcome reflection.

It should not issue country-specific operational contracts outside its authority.

## Event and interaction hooks

- Forum announced;
- charter draft published;
- negotiation delayed;
- delegation withdrawal;
- emergency session;
- resolution announced;
- procedural challenge;
- public correction.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through:

- accurate summaries;
- balanced participation;
- timely corrections;
- visible conflict-of-interest controls;
- decisions that follow published procedure.

Trust falls through:

- hidden rule changes;
- misleading claims of consensus;
- information leaks;
- unexplained delay during crisis;
- favoritism toward large contributors.

## Failure and retirement behavior

If the Forum loses legitimacy during a session, the arc may continue through bilateral or regional negotiations. The definition remains available for other sessions.

Retirement requires a replacement institution or explicit removal of dependent arcs and contracts.

## Validation

- no executive power implied beyond mandate;
- all issued contracts are global, procedural, analytical, or reflective;
- public statements match recorded session state;
- lead character authority remains consistent;
- Forum trust, if not mechanically supported, remains narrative metadata.
