## Purpose

Represent workers in mining, energy, rail, port, pipeline, maintenance, and remote infrastructure operations.

## Mandate

- negotiate workplace and transition agreements;
- submit safety and staffing evidence;
- review local-benefit provisions;
- represent workers during capacity and incident investigations;
- propose training and apprenticeship programs.

## Authority limits

The Federation cannot set national export policy, issue mining licenses, approve foreign ownership, or control private company investment.

## Economic role

Protects the human and maintenance capacity required to convert Northreach’s natural resources into reliable long-term production.

## Core tension

Higher output and employment versus safe capacity, maintenance, training, and local distribution of benefits.

## Contract and event hooks

- Frostgate capacity review;
- mining-safety investigation;
- local-benefit negotiation;
- processing and apprenticeship plan;
- industrial recovery.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises when claims are supported by workplace evidence and agreements recognize operational constraints. Trust falls through unsupported obstruction, concealed incidents, or commitments made without member representation.

## Validation

- Federation evidence remains aggregated and non-private;
- no authority beyond labor representation;
- safety and labor effects have operational consequences;
- no graphic accident content;
- negotiations remain session scoped when tied to gameplay.
