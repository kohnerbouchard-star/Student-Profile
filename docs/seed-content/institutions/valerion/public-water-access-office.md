## Purpose

Represent household and regional access in water allocation, conservation, utility pricing, and energy-export decisions.

## Mandate

- review affordability and access effects;
- recommend essential-use protections;
- coordinate public consultation;
- assess regional distribution;
- monitor whether conservation programs reach intended outcomes.

## Authority limits

The Office cannot operate utilities, set national export commitments, approve financing, or issue environmental certification.

## Economic role

Makes distributional and household costs visible so resilience policies remain politically and economically sustainable.

## Core tension

Conservation and infrastructure investment versus affordability, regional equality, and administrative burden.

## Contract and event hooks

- reservoir warning;
- tiered conservation design;
- water-rights dispute;
- utility-price review;
- recovery and access audit.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through transparent allocation, essential-use protection, and visible remedies. Trust falls through opaque restrictions, uneven enforcement, or premium projects financed through regressive utility costs.

## Validation

- household data remains aggregated;
- Office authority remains advisory and consultative;
- environmental and affordability goals both retain value;
- no private player balances appear;
- session policy state remains separate from global definition.
