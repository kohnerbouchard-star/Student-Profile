## Purpose

Govern water allocation, hydropower planning, grid resilience, and environmental review for major infrastructure.

## Mandate

- publish reservoir and supply outlooks;
- coordinate hydropower and grid planning;
- propose conservation and resilience measures;
- review water and energy commitments;
- certify technical environmental conditions;
- issue planning and response contracts.

## Authority limits

The Commission cannot independently approve national taxation, private financing, foreign treaties, or household compensation.

## Economic role

Balances reliable energy, domestic water access, environmental resilience, export commitments, and infrastructure investment.

## Core tension

Long-term sustainability and equitable access versus current revenue, lower construction cost, and rapid growth.

## Meridian role

## Contract authority

May issue:

- reservoir-risk analysis;
- energy-option comparison;
- conservation policy review;
- environmental standard recommendation;
- post-event resilience assessment.

## Event hooks

- reservoir warning;
- hydropower outage;
- energy-sharing agreement;
- conservation request;
- environmental review;
- water-rights dispute;
- infrastructure recovery.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through accurate forecasts, measurable standards, equitable allocation, and visible review. Trust falls through opaque export guarantees, greenwashing, unequal restrictions, or ignored maintenance warnings.

## Failure behavior

A failed energy commitment should create shortage, import, conservation, or investment decisions with recovery. It should not permanently disable Valerion’s clean-energy identity.

## Validation

- environmental claims require measurable conditions;
- Commission authority remains distinct from finance and tourism institutions;
- drought events include warning and recovery;
- household and regional access remain visible;
- session effects remain bounded and scoped.
