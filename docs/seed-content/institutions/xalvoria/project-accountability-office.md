## Purpose

Provide independent review of major project assumptions, financing terms, procurement, conflicts of interest, milestones, and public-value claims.

## Mandate

- audit disclosed project records;
- test downside scenarios;
- publish material findings;
- recommend milestone and disclosure controls;
- review restructuring and conflicts of interest;
- explain financing terms in accessible language.

## Authority limits

The Office cannot approve or cancel projects alone, set monetary policy, seize assets, or access private player holdings.

## Economic role

Reduces hidden risk and principal-agent problems while adding review time, staffing cost, and possible delay.

## Core tension

Rapid execution and confidentiality versus transparency, realistic forecasting, borrower autonomy, and public value.

## Contract and event hooks

- Meridian term-sheet review;
- project overrun;
- conflict-of-interest allegation;
- foreign restructuring;
- completion audit.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through evidence, materiality standards, timely review, and visible correction. Trust falls through political targeting, selective disclosure, or audits used to block competition without evidence.

## Validation

- allegations and findings remain distinct;
- Office authority remains review and recommendation;
- finance retains real benefits and costs;
- private player data is excluded;
- session project records remain separate from global definition.
