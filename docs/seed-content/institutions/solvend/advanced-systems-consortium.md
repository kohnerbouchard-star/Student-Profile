## Purpose

Coordinate university and industry research for advanced logistics, aerospace, AI, precision systems, and Corridor technology standards.

## Mandate

- organize joint research programs;
- propose interoperability standards;
- license consortium technology;
- evaluate technical readiness;
- coordinate public and private research partners;
- publish non-sensitive performance findings.

## Authority limits

The Consortium cannot approve national export controls, compel patent licensing, authorize foreign ownership, or deploy security access outside contractual authority.

## Economic role

Transforms education, research, and imported strategic inputs into high-value intellectual property, systems, and technical services.

## Core tension

Rapid funding and shared deployment versus research independence, intellectual-property protection, and long-term domestic value.

## Meridian role

Designs optimization, monitoring, maintenance, and interoperability systems for `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`.

Lead character: `character.solvend.sena-oris.v1`.

## Contract authority

May issue:

- technology-option comparison;
- interoperability analysis;
- research ownership review;
- component-risk assessment;
- pilot validation contract.

## Event hooks

- prototype success;
- patent dispute;
- research grant;
- component delay;
- foreign acquisition offer;
- integrity review;
- skilled-labor shortage.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through validated claims, transparent licensing, independent review, and successful delivery. Trust falls through exaggerated forecasts, hidden ownership terms, unreproducible results, or poor handling of data and security.

## Failure behavior

A failed pilot should delay or narrow deployment, create revision work, and affect confidence. It should not automatically erase all research value or permanently disable Solvend.

## Validation

- technical claims remain fictional and non-operational;
- patents and ownership use economic concepts, not legal guarantees;
- performance forecasts are distinguished from verified results;
- event effects remain session scoped;
- consortium authority does not override national government or company ownership.
