Owner domain: world and narrative canon

## Purpose

Assess and respond to threats against critical networks, customs systems, payment verification, data routes, and digital infrastructure.

## Mandate

- publish threat assessments;
- coordinate authorized incident response;
- request time-limited technical access;
- support containment and recovery;
- share indicators with participating institutions;
- review infrastructure resilience.

## Authority limits

The Directorate cannot claim permanent access by default, determine criminal guilt, control foreign systems without authorization, or suppress independent review.

## Economic role

Protects transaction integrity, network uptime, trade verification, fintech access, and confidence in digital infrastructure.

## Core tension

Fast and effective security access versus privacy, national control, independent oversight, and concentration risk.

## Meridian role

Leads the technical response to `event.meridian.customs-security-intrusion.v1` within `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`.

Lead character: `character.syndalis.neris-vale.v1`.

## Contract authority

May issue:

- threat-impact analysis;
- security architecture comparison;
- recovery prioritization;
- access-control recommendation;
- post-incident review.

## Event hooks

- intrusion warning;
- containment success;
- disputed attribution;
- data breach;
- cable outage;
- access dispute;
- independent audit.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through accurate threat reporting, limited access, expiry, audit, and effective recovery. Trust falls through overreach, unsupported attribution, concealed monitoring, or failure to disclose material incidents.

## Failure behavior

A failed response should create delay, manual alternatives, federated recovery, and trust consequences. It must not expose real credentials, tactical attack details, or private player data.

## Validation

- cyber content remains non-tactical;
- attribution confidence is explicit;
- access is bounded and session-specific;
- no private or ownership identifiers appear in copy;
- security benefits and concentration costs are both represented.
