## Purpose

Review data-access, emergency authorization, platform, privacy, audit, and remedy disputes involving Syndalian systems and institutions.

## Mandate

- review access requests and complaints;
- define scope, purpose, expiry, logging, and remedy conditions;
- order or recommend independent audit within its fictional mandate;
- publish decisions and corrections;
- review whether emergency access should end or narrow.

## Authority limits

The Tribunal cannot conduct operational cyber response, direct foreign systems, determine criminal guilt outside its mandate, or access private player credentials and data.

## Economic role

Creates trust and predictable limits for data and security services, while adding review time and possible constraints during fast-moving incidents.

## Core tension

Effective emergency access and shared visibility versus privacy, national control, concentration, and permanent institutional overreach.

## Contract and event hooks

- Meridian security-access request;
- audit dispute;
- attribution correction;
- platform complaint;
- post-incident remedy.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through clear reasoning, automatic expiry, enforceable audit, and correction. Trust falls through secret standards, indefinite authorization, political favoritism, or decisions unsupported by the record.

## Validation

- no tactical cyber detail;
- no private credentials or player data;
- emergency powers have scope and expiry;
- Tribunal authority remains review and remedy, not operations;
- security and privacy costs are both represented.
