## Purpose

Coordinate strategic manufacturing, state procurement, industrial employment, export commitments, and modernization policy.

## Mandate

- propose industrial procurement;
- coordinate state-enterprise capacity;
- negotiate production commitments;
- monitor industrial employment;
- recommend modernization and component strategy;
- issue industrial-analysis and response contracts.

## Authority limits

The Ministry cannot ignore independent safety findings, create unlimited fiscal support, control foreign buyers, or guarantee that every state enterprise remains open.

## Economic role

Turns industrial capacity and workforce specialization into physical infrastructure, exports, employment, and strategic resilience.

## Core tension

Employment and production scale versus debt, efficiency, energy use, maintenance, and trade dependency.

## Meridian role

Offers steel, rail, vehicle, and heavy-equipment procurement for `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`.

Lead character: `character.dravenlok.mara-volsk.v1`.

## Contract authority

May issue:

- capacity audit;
- procurement comparison;
- component-allocation plan;
- modernization proposal;
- industrial-employment transition plan.

## Event hooks

- procurement guarantee;
- factory expansion;
- component shortage;
- energy shock;
- accident;
- debt warning;
- restructuring milestone.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through realistic commitments, maintenance, safety, and credible worker transition. Trust falls through concealed debt, unsafe expansion, political favoritism, or promises unsupported by capacity.

## Failure behavior

A missed commitment should create delay, substitution, restructuring, and reputational effects. It should not remove all industrial capacity or permanently block Dravenlok from future contracts.

## Validation

- industrial and defense content remains economic and non-tactical;
- safety authority remains independent;
- employment effects are not used as automatic justification for every subsidy;
- procurement events remain session scoped;
- company and ministry authority remain separate.
