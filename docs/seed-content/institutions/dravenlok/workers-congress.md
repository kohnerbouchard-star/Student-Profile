## Purpose

Represent industrial workers and communities during procurement, modernization, safety, restructuring, training, and plant-transition decisions.

## Mandate

- negotiate labor and transition agreements;
- submit safety and maintenance evidence;
- propose apprenticeships and retraining;
- review community employment effects;
- participate in restructuring and recovery plans.

## Authority limits

The Congress cannot set national procurement, guarantee every facility remains open, manage companies, or override independent safety findings.

## Economic role

Supports stable skilled labor and credible transition, reducing the social and operational cost of industrial modernization while increasing short-term program cost.

## Core tension

Employment continuity and bargaining power versus productivity, debt, safety, and the need to retire unsustainable capacity.

## Contract and event hooks

- Ironhold guarantee;
- factory safety warning;
- apprenticeship expansion;
- modernization agreement;
- restructuring and community recovery.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through credible member evidence, funded agreements, and willingness to address unsafe or insolvent operations. Trust falls through unsupported obstruction, hidden incidents, or promises that cannot be financed.

## Validation

- workers retain agency and expertise;
- no graphic incident content;
- Congress authority remains representational and negotiating;
- employment benefits do not erase debt or safety cost;
- player and employee private information remains excluded.
