## Purpose

Review cargo-record, operator, safety, and commercial-recognition reforms needed for durable access to regulated trade.

## Mandate

- conduct public and independent audits;
- recommend proportional compliance standards;
- publish corrections and reform progress;
- negotiate recognition frameworks;
- review operator remediation.

## Authority limits

The Commission cannot prosecute crimes, guarantee foreign recognition, operate ports, or determine insurer pricing.

## Economic role

Builds reputation and information quality that can reduce insurance cost, expand market access, and protect legitimate repair and secondary-market activity.

## Core tension

Stronger verification and recognition versus cost, delay, operator concentration, and loss of flexibility.

## Contract and event hooks

- Dusk Harbor audit;
- operator review;
- route-recognition negotiation;
- public correction;
- reform backlash.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through evidence, due process, proportionate remedies, and visible progress. Trust falls through selective enforcement, hidden findings, or standards designed only for large incumbents.

## Validation

- allegations and confirmed findings remain distinct;
- informal commerce is not automatically illegal;
- Commission authority remains investigative and recommendatory;
- small-operator transition costs are visible;
- session events do not rewrite global institution identity.
