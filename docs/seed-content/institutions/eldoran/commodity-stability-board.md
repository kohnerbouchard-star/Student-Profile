## Purpose

Monitor food supply, harvest conditions, strategic reserves, commodity prices, and emergency procurement.

## Mandate

- publish harvest and price outlooks;
- advise on reserve policy;
- coordinate market and logistics data;
- recommend emergency imports or distribution actions;
- issue food-security analysis contracts;
- review recovery after supply shocks.

## Authority limits

The Board cannot independently set retail prices, order export restrictions, authorize fiscal subsidies, or control private producers without separate legal authority.

## Economic role

Reduces information gaps and helps balance producer incentives, household affordability, reserve readiness, and international commitments.

## Core tension

Domestic affordability and resilience versus producer income, export revenue, and market incentives.

## Meridian role

Assesses harvest and food-distribution pressure in `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`.

Lead character: `character.eldoran.halden-marr.v1`.

## Contract authority

May issue:

- harvest analysis;
- reserve-option comparison;
- logistics prioritization;
- food-price response recommendation;
- post-policy outcome review.

## Event hooks

- harvest revision;
- reserve release;
- commodity panic;
- export pressure;
- warehouse shortage;
- rail disruption;
- forecast correction.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises when forecasts are transparent, corrections are timely, and household and producer effects are both acknowledged. Trust falls through concealed shortages, politicized forecasts, or unexplained reserve decisions.

## Failure behavior

An inaccurate forecast should generate correction, confidence effects, and review. It should not retroactively rewrite prior market or player actions.

## Validation

- forecast and confirmed outcome are distinct;
- reserve actions are bounded and non-repeatable without replenishment;
- Board authority does not imply control over all prices;
- food-security copy is age appropriate;
- session effects do not alter global definitions.
