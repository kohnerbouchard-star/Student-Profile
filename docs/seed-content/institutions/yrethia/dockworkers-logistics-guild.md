## Purpose

Represent terminal operators, equipment technicians, maintenance crews, dispatchers, and logistics workers across Sableport.

## Mandate

- negotiate staffing and transition agreements;
- submit operational capacity evidence;
- review automation and safety plans;
- coordinate training;
- participate in incident and maintenance review.

## Authority limits

The Guild cannot allocate berths, set customs rules, determine insurance coverage, or approve national trade agreements.

## Economic role

Provides the skilled operational capacity that makes port infrastructure usable rather than merely installed.

## Core tension

Higher throughput and automation versus staffing, maintenance, safe workloads, and transition costs.

## Contract and event hooks

- Sableport capacity warning;
- automation transition;
- staffing shortfall;
- maintenance recovery;
- negotiated rerouting support.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through accurate operational evidence and workable agreements. Trust falls through concealed capacity, politically timed disruptions, or refusal to recognize successful transition investment.

## Validation

- labor evidence remains aggregated;
- automation retains benefits and costs;
- no assumption that all Guild actions represent all workers unanimously;
- no authority beyond representation and negotiation;
- session-scoped event state remains separate from global institution definition.
