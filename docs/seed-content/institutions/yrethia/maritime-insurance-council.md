## Purpose

Coordinate shipping-risk classification, coverage standards, claims reporting, and Corridor insurance requirements.

## Mandate

- define risk classes;
- publish coverage conditions;
- coordinate industry loss data;
- evaluate route and cargo controls;
- issue warnings when risks are underpriced;
- support emergency coverage frameworks.

## Authority limits

The Council cannot operate ports, enforce customs law, order cargo seizure, or guarantee coverage without participating insurers.

## Economic role

Converts uncertain shipping risk into priced commercial terms. Its standards may increase short-term cost while reducing severe loss and improving confidence.

## Core tension

Throughput and commercial opportunity versus underwriting discipline and operational safety.

## Meridian role

Evaluates Sableport capacity, Thaloris routing, and digital customs risk in `story-arc.global.meridian-corridor.v1`.

Lead character: `character.yrethia.mira-sen.v1`.

## Contract authority

May issue:

- route-risk analysis;
- insurance standard comparison;
- cargo-priority recommendation;
- loss-scenario review;
- emergency coverage proposal.

## Event hooks

- major insured loss;
- premium increase;
- route reclassification;
- coverage withdrawal;
- successful safety reform;
- customs-data incident;
- capacity warning.

## Trust conditions

Trust rises through transparent criteria, timely claims, and evidence-based warnings. Trust falls if the Council protects incumbents, conceals losses, or changes standards after an incident without explanation.

## Failure behavior

Coverage withdrawal should create rerouting, delay, higher cost, or alternate financing—not erase cargo or create irreversible session failure.

## Validation

- risk direction matches actual event exposure;
- premiums and losses are not treated as guaranteed company profit or loss;
- no claim that the Council controls all insurers;
- uncertain events use appropriate confidence language;
- Council actions remain session scoped.
