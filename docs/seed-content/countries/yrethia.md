# Yrethia Seed Content Profile

Status: draft foundation
Canonical key: `YRETHIA`
Stable country ID: `country.global.yrethia.v1`
Capital: Sableport
Currency: YRC — Yrethian Crown
Working adjective: Yrethian
Working demonym: Yrethian

## Player-facing promise

Yrethia offers a trade, insurance, logistics, and regulatory strategy. Players should feel that reliable rules and commercial infrastructure create value, but that high standards can become slow, costly, and politically contested when the world needs rapid action.

## Structural strengths

1. Major automated container-port capacity.
2. Strong maritime insurance and freight finance.
3. Credible customs, arbitration, and commercial law.
4. Logistics software and port-automation expertise.
5. International reputation for predictable trade.

## Structural vulnerabilities

1. Dependence on global trade volume.
2. Exposure to congestion, strikes, shipping accidents, and cyber disruption.
3. High sensitivity to war-risk and insurance losses.
4. Limited tolerance for gray-market or poorly documented cargo.
5. Domestic concern that trade benefits concentrate around Sableport.

## External dependencies

- Eldoran and Dravenlok cargo volume.
- Northreach resources and energy shipments.
- Syndalis data and network security.
- Thaloris overflow, repair, and emergency routing.
- Valerion and Xalvoria finance flows.

Yrethia is primarily a throughput and service economy rather than a commodity producer.

Positive exposure:

- broad trade volume;
- shipping-rate increases when capacity remains operational;
- demand for risk management.

Negative exposure:

- cargo-volume collapse;
- persistent congestion;
- insured-loss concentration.

## Core institutions

### Maritime Insurance Council

Coordinates risk classification, coverage standards, and loss reporting.

### Sableport Port Authority

Owns capacity planning, berth allocation, automation, safety, and labor coordination.

### Yrethian Customs and Trade Commission

Owns cargo compliance, digital documentation, tariff processing, and trusted-trader status.

### Dockworkers and Logistics Guild

Represents port labor, operators, and maintenance specialists.

### Sable Commercial Court

Provides arbitration and enforceability for major cross-border contracts.

## Meridian Corridor role

Yrethia wants Sableport to become the primary regulated maritime gateway.

National objective:

Capture long-term trade volume while preserving insurance, customs, and legal credibility.

Primary decisions:

- strict compliance versus rapid emergency routing;
- automation versus labor and resilience investment;
- centralized customs data versus federated verification;
- Sableport priority versus multi-port competition.

## Early country arc: The Sableport Capacity Ceiling

Setup:

Corridor forecasts push Sableport close to maximum sustainable throughput.

Escalation:

Congestion increases turnaround times, while insurers warn that fatigue and maintenance delays are raising operational risk.

Decision options:

1. Expand capacity using Xalvorian finance.
2. Redirect selected cargo through Thaloris.
3. Raise port fees and ration low-priority cargo.
4. Slow Corridor commitments until maintenance is complete.

Consequences:

- trade volume;
- insurance cost;
- labor relations;
- debt exposure;
- Thaloris relationship;
- reputation.

## Transformation arc: Rules Under Pressure

Question:

Can Yrethia remain a trusted rules-based trade hub while adapting to crises that reward flexibility?

Possible outcomes:

- modernized high-trust hub;
- expensive and exclusionary trade gate;
- cooperative two-port corridor with Thaloris;
- weakened credibility after exceptions and scandal;
- resilient decentralized customs network.

## Balance risks

- Yrethia should not profit automatically from every increase in trade or disruption.
- Insurance gains from higher premiums must be offset by claims risk.
- Port capacity should create choices, not a permanent throughput penalty.
- Compliance should generate value, not only costs.
- Thaloris cooperation should be a genuine option rather than always inferior.
