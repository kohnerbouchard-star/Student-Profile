# Dravenlok Seed Content Profile

Status: draft foundation
Canonical key: `DRAVENLOK`
Stable country ID: `country.global.dravenlok.v1`
Capital: Ironhold
Currency: DRV — Dravenlok Vek
Working adjective: Dravenlok
Working demonym: Draven

## Player-facing promise

Dravenlok offers an industrial capacity, employment, manufacturing, rail, and strategic-production strategy. Players should feel able to build physical systems at scale while managing energy intensity, imported inputs, state-enterprise debt, trade restrictions, and the pressure to protect employment even when older production becomes inefficient.

## Structural strengths

1. Large steel, machinery, rail, and vehicle production base.
2. Skilled industrial workforce and deep supplier networks.
3. State capacity for coordinated procurement and rapid production expansion.
4. Defense and heavy-logistics capability.
5. Existing factories, foundries, depots, and export terminals.

## Structural vulnerabilities

1. High energy intensity.
2. Dependence on imported food, advanced components, and software.
3. Exposure to sanctions, export restrictions, and foreign-market access.
4. State-enterprise debt and inefficient legacy capacity.
5. Environmental, safety, and maintenance liabilities.

## External dependencies

- Northreach energy and minerals.
- Eldoran food.
- Solvend software, advanced components, and precision systems.
- Yrethian shipping and insurance.
- Xalvorian project finance and foreign construction demand.

## Stabilizers

- public procurement;
- long-lived industrial assets;
- workforce experience;

- ability to substitute domestic production for selected imports.

Minister for Industrial Coordination. Forceful, production-focused, skeptical of plans that do not create physical capacity.

Working concept: Workers Congress bargaining chair.

## Meridian Corridor role

Dravenlok supplies steel, rail systems, vehicles, heavy equipment, and construction capacity.

National objective:

Secure long-term procurement that sustains employment and modernization while reducing dependence on restricted foreign components and markets.

Primary decisions:

- guaranteed orders versus competitive procurement;
- rapid factory expansion versus maintenance;
- domestic substitution versus imported efficiency;
- employment protection versus restructuring;
- defense-capable production versus civilian infrastructure priority.

## Early country arc: The Ironhold Guarantee

Setup:

Dravenlok offers discounted Meridian rail equipment in exchange for guaranteed purchase volumes.

Escalation:

A capacity audit finds that several factories require maintenance and imported control systems to meet the promised schedule.

Decision options:

1. Accept the guarantee and finance modernization.
2. Reduce the guaranteed volume.
3. subcontract advanced components to Solvend.
4. prioritize employment and use existing capacity.
5. open procurement to foreign competitors.

Consequences:

- employment;
- debt;
- schedule;
- safety;
- trade dependency;
- company outlook.

## Transformation arc: The Modernization Bargain

Question:

Can Dravenlok modernize heavy industry without abandoning industrial communities or locking the country into inefficient state support?

Possible outcomes:

- modern competitive industrial base;
- protected but fiscally costly legacy system;
- specialized strategic manufacturer;
- severe contraction followed by retraining;
- export revival through multinational partnerships.

## Balance risks

- Sanctions should not permanently suppress all gameplay.
- State support needs fiscal and efficiency costs.
- Restructuring must include recovery and worker outcomes.
- Industrial scale should be valuable but energy and input intensive.
