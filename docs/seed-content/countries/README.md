# Country Seed Content Catalog

Status: foundation in progress

Each country has a separate file because country identity is referenced by companies, events, contracts, map locations, institutions, characters, currencies, market exposure, and story arcs.

## Country-file template

Every country file should define:

1. Canonical identity
2. Adjective and demonym
3. Capital and major locations
4. Country promise to the player
5. Structural economic strengths
6. Structural vulnerabilities
7. External dependencies
8. Stabilizers
9. Crisis amplifiers
10. Currency identity and sensitivities
11. Primary and secondary industries
12. Commodity exposures
13. National institutions
14. Recurring characters
15. Company directions
16. Country story arcs
17. Global arc role
18. Event catalog directions
19. Contract catalog directions
20. Store and banking directions
21. Progression and achievements
22. Location catalog
23. News themes
24. Player decision themes
25. Balance risks
26. Classroom learning objectives
27. Sensitive-content constraints
28. Technical compatibility questions
29. Definition of country readiness

## Country readiness minimum

A country is not production-ready until it has:

- one approved identity;
- one official currency mapping;
- at least five meaningful locations;
- at least two recurring perspectives from different institution types;
- approximately three companies with different risk profiles;
- one introductory contract;
- one development contract chain;
- at least five event hooks;
- one early story arc;
- one crisis or transformation arc;
- banking and store availability rules;
- explicit external dependencies;
- economic and narrative balance review;
- technical references mapped to the authoritative country key.

## Shared balance rule

Every country must have a viable opening path, but viability does not mean identical conditions. Country strengths should create distinct opportunities, while vulnerabilities create different forms of risk.

No country may be:

- best at all economic dimensions;
- dependent on one unavoidable random event;
- permanently disadvantaged by assignment;
- represented as inherently dishonest, violent, backward, or morally inferior;
- reduced to one real-world national analogy;
- given a unique mechanic that the runtime system cannot support for other countries where fairness requires parity.
