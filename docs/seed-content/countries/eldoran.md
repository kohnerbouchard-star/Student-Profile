# Eldoran Seed Content Profile

Status: draft foundation
Canonical key: `ELDORAN`
Stable country ID: `country.global.eldoran.v1`
Capital: Crescent Bay
Currency: ELD — Eldoran Ducat
Working adjective: Eldoran
Working demonym: Eldoran

## Player-facing promise

Eldoran offers a food, commodity, distribution, and stability strategy. Players should feel that the country is indispensable to ordinary economic life, but must manage the conflict between profitable exports, domestic affordability, storage, transport, weather risk, and public expectations.

## Structural strengths

1. Fertile agricultural regions and food-processing capacity.
2. Central geographic position and inland logistics.
3. Wholesale markets and commodity-pricing institutions.
4. Rail, freight, storage, and grain-terminal infrastructure.
5. Broad domestic consumer and producer base.

## Structural vulnerabilities

1. Harvest and weather exposure.
2. Food-price inflation and public sensitivity.
3. Rail, warehouse, and transport bottlenecks.
4. Currency pressure when imports or commodity markets destabilize.
5. Tension between producer income and consumer affordability.

## External dependencies

- Northreach energy and selected agricultural inputs.
- Yrethian and Thalorian export routes.
- Dravenlok machinery and rail equipment.
- Valerion water and clean-energy systems.
- Xalvorian infrastructure capital.

## Stabilizers

- diversified agricultural regions;
- strategic reserves;
- central wholesale markets;
- broad transport network;
- food-processing and storage capacity;
- defensive consumer demand.

## Crisis amplifiers

- simultaneous crop failure and rail disruption;
- speculative commodity panic;
- inadequate storage;
- export commitments during domestic shortage;
- currency depreciation increasing farm-input costs;
- poorly targeted subsidies.

## Meridian Corridor role

Eldoran supplies food, central rail connections, wholesale markets, and commodity terminals.

National objective:

Use the Corridor to improve distribution and producer access without causing domestic shortages or unaffordable food prices.

Primary decisions:

- reserve release versus market adjustment;
- domestic supply priority versus export commitments;
- consumer subsidy versus producer investment;
- emergency imports versus long-term productivity;
- centralized rail priority versus market allocation.

## Early country arc: The Harvest Revision

Setup:

The Commodity Stability Board lowers the annual harvest estimate.

Escalation:

Export commitments remain high while rail maintenance limits redistribution between regions.

Decision options:

1. Release strategic reserves.
2. Restrict selected exports.
3. Subsidize emergency imports.
4. Support consumers directly while preserving market prices.
5. Invest in logistics and accept short-term price pressure.

Consequences:

- food prices;
- fiscal cost;
- producer income;
- trade relationships;
- confidence;
- reserve levels.

## Transformation arc: The Price of Stability

Question:

Can Eldoran remain the world’s food and commodity anchor while modernizing production and protecting households?

Possible outcomes:

- resilient high-productivity food system;
- export-led producer boom with affordability pressure;
- heavily controlled market with lower investment;
- improved targeted support;
- prolonged food and logistics instability.

## Balance risks

- Eldoran’s centrality should not make it universally powerful or universally punished.
- Food events require clear timing and recovery.
- Export restrictions should have foreign and domestic trade-offs.
- Subsidies must have visible fiscal costs.
- Reserve release cannot be infinitely repeatable.

## Sensitive-content constraints

- food insecurity should be handled seriously and without humiliation;
- avoid graphic disaster content;
- represent farmers, workers, consumers, and market institutions as distinct interests;
- avoid implying that affordability concerns are economically irrational.

## Technical questions

- Can reserves exist as an indicator or only story metadata?
- Are commodity and food-price events supported by the current market model?
- Can contracts react to current event state?
- Can the system represent targeted versus broad support mechanically?
- Are rail and warehouse capacity location concepts supported or planned?
