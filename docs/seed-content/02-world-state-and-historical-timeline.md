# World State and Historical Timeline

Status: draft foundation
Narrative epoch: Meridian Transition

## Purpose

Define the shared historical background and exact starting conditions for a new Econovaria game session. This file separates durable world canon from mutable session state.

## Durable world premise

Econovaria developed as a network of ten specialized economies. Geography, resources, institutions, technology, and political choices made each country unusually capable in a limited set of domains. Over time, specialization created prosperity and systemic interdependence.

No country can maintain modern living standards without external inputs:

- Northreach controls strategic resources and northern access but depends on technology, food, and foreign capital.
- Solvend creates high-value research and advanced systems but depends on imported resources and continued funding.
- Yrethia provides regulated trade and insurance but depends on open shipping lanes and global demand.
- Thaloris absorbs disruption and irregular trade but depends on tolerance from insurers, regulators, and neighbors.
- Eldoran anchors food and commodity distribution but remains exposed to harvests, transport capacity, and inflation.
- Valerion supplies clean energy and premium services but depends on water, confidence, and its environmental reputation.
- Lumenor supplies legitimacy, education, and diplomacy but depends on institutional trust and public funding.
- Xalvoria supplies capital and project finance but is exposed to defaults, political backlash, and concentration of influence.
- Dravenlok supplies heavy industry but depends on energy, food imports, advanced components, and export access.
- Syndalis supplies data infrastructure and cybersecurity but depends on trust, regulation, and uninterrupted network access.

The central design rule is that strength and vulnerability come from the same specialization.

## Historical eras

### Era I: Consolidation of the Ten States

Approximate purpose: explain modern borders and national institutions.

Key developments:

- Regional authorities consolidated transport, taxation, and currency systems.
- Major capitals became administrative and commercial centers.
- Early rail, port, energy, and communications systems were built primarily for domestic needs.
- National currencies replaced fragmented regional instruments.
- Governments developed distinct approaches to state ownership, finance, trade, and public services.

Gameplay legacy:

- strong national identities;
- country-specific regulations;
- different infrastructure ownership models;
- persistent domestic concern over sovereignty.

### Era II: The Interdependence Acceleration

Key developments:

- Yrethian port modernization reduced trade costs.
- Eldoran’s wholesale markets became regional pricing references.
- Xalvorian capital financed cross-border infrastructure.
- Solvend research increased the value of Northreach minerals.
- Dravenlok industrial production expanded across regional supply chains.
- Valerion exported clean-energy and water-management expertise.
- Lumenor became the preferred site for international agreements.
- Syndalis built critical financial and data-routing infrastructure.
- Thaloris expanded repair, re-export, and bonded-warehouse capacity around disrupted trade.

Gameplay legacy:

- cross-country sector exposure;
- multinational companies and supply agreements;
- trade-route sensitivity;
- shared infrastructure dependencies.

### Era III: The First Systems Shock

A combined period of shipping disruption, commodity inflation, and financial stress exposed weaknesses in the interconnected system.

Key developments:

- Eldoran food prices rose sharply after poor harvests and transport bottlenecks.
- Northreach restricted selected strategic exports.
- Yrethian shipping insurance costs increased.
- Thaloris gained trade volume but faced regulatory scrutiny.
- Dravenlok expanded state industrial support.
- Xalvoria restructured several foreign infrastructure loans.
- Syndalis experienced a major trust crisis after a data-routing controversy.
- Lumenor coordinated a temporary stabilization framework.

Gameplay legacy:

- strategic reserves;
- stronger customs and compliance systems;
- public skepticism toward foreign dependency;
- pressure for resilient infrastructure.

### Era IV: The Quiet Recovery

The ten countries avoided systemic collapse but did not resolve underlying tensions.

Key developments:

- growth returned unevenly;
- governments accumulated more debt;
- technology investment accelerated;
- public concern about cost of living increased;
- companies shortened some supply chains;
- infrastructure maintenance lagged behind expansion;
- international cooperation became more procedural and less trusted.

Gameplay legacy:

- positive but fragile growth;
- politically sensitive interest rates;
- infrastructure backlogs;
- demand for new investment;
- competing resilience strategies.

### Era V: The Meridian Transition

Current era and default game opening.

The proposed Meridian Corridor would connect rail, ports, energy, data, customs, commodity terminals, and research facilities. It is the largest coordinated economic proposal since the First Systems Shock.

It offers a route to higher productivity and resilience, but the countries disagree over ownership, financing, data access, environmental rules, labor standards, and national control.

## Default starting-world condition

The following describes the narrative and directional state, not final numeric values.

### Global economy

- Growth: positive, slowing.
- Inflation: above preferred levels, driven by food, housing, logistics, and selected industrial inputs.
- Employment: generally stable, with regional weakness in legacy industries and youth employment.
- Interest rates: moderately restrictive.
- Investment: strong in technology and strategic infrastructure; weaker in ordinary maintenance.
- Confidence: cautious.
- Trade: high volume, limited spare capacity.
- Energy: stable supply with localized water and transmission risks.
- Food: adequate global supply with rising price pressure.
- Finance: liquid but increasingly selective.
- Cyber risk: elevated.

### Political economy

- Governments want growth but fear inflation and dependency.
- Voters and institutions are more skeptical of opaque foreign financing.
- Businesses want faster infrastructure decisions.
- Labor groups want employment guarantees and wage protection.
- Environmental institutions want binding safeguards.
- Security institutions want control over strategic data and resources.
- Smaller countries want access without domination.

## Default session-opening tensions

A new game may start with all or a selected subset of these tensions:

1. Meridian Corridor negotiations begin in Starfall.
2. Eldoran issues a lower harvest forecast.
3. Sableport reports capacity pressure.
4. Northreach reviews strategic mineral export terms.
5. Solvend faces a skilled-labor shortage.
6. Valerion reservoir levels are below seasonal expectations.
7. Xalvoria offers rapid financing with asset-control clauses.
8. Dravenlok requests guaranteed industrial procurement.
9. Syndalis warns of attacks against customs infrastructure.
10. Thaloris proposes emergency routing through Dusk Harbor.

These are content triggers. A runtime session may activate, delay, omit, or vary them according to the approved scenario profile.

## Session scenario profiles

The production pack should support distinct opening profiles without rewriting canon.

### Standard classroom profile

- moderate volatility;
- all countries viable;
- clear learning signals;
- limited simultaneous crises;
- guided Meridian arc.

### Market-intensive profile

- more company and commodity events;
- higher price sensitivity;
- reduced narrative guidance;
- greater portfolio risk.

### Policy-intensive profile

- more country decisions and public trade-offs;
- slower market effects;
- stronger reputation and institutional consequences.

### Crisis-response profile

- multiple connected disruptions;
- emergency contracts;
- constrained resources;
- shorter decision windows;
- explicit recovery objective.

### Sandbox profile

- major arcs optional;
- broad content access;
- instructor-defined conditions;
- no assumption that the Meridian arc is active.

## Continuity requirements

- Historical facts do not change between sessions unless the content pack version changes.
- Starting conditions may vary by scenario profile.
- Active events and outcomes are session-specific.
- A completed story outcome in one session never changes another session.
- News copy must distinguish history, current condition, forecast, allegation, and confirmed outcome.
- Characters must not refer to an event as resolved before the runtime state records resolution.
- Follow-up arcs must reference the actual selected outcome, not a presumed canonical choice.
