## Purpose

Present a resource-access negotiation in which reliable supply, investment, reserves, processing, and ownership cannot all be maximized simultaneously.

## Opening message

“Northreach can support new Meridian mineral volumes, but not under an open-ended access arrangement. We require exact terms covering reserves, ownership, processing, infrastructure, and review.”

## Response options

### A. Long-term supply with ownership safeguards

- Benefit: predictable supply and investment planning.
- Cost: reduced pricing flexibility and long commitment.
- Immediate reply: Darek requests a defined volume, review period, and prohibition on automatic asset transfer.
- Narrative effect: supports diversified controlled partnership.

### B. Reduced commitment and larger reserve

- Benefit: stronger national buffer and bargaining power.
- Cost: slower Corridor construction and lower current revenue.
- Immediate reply: Darek accepts a smaller initial program and asks partners to revise schedules.
- Narrative effect: sovereignty position strengthened.

### C. Domestic processing requirement

- Benefit: more value retained in Northreach.
- Cost: capital, time, energy, and technical dependency.
- Immediate reply: Darek requests a joint feasibility study with Solvend.
- Narrative effect: unlocks processing and technology follow-up.

### D. Broad foreign investment access

- Benefit: fastest capacity growth.
- Cost: ownership, concentration, and political risk.
- Immediate reply: Darek records the proposal but requires formal national review; he does not approve it personally.
- Narrative effect: finance-first path support with sovereignty warning.

## No-response behavior

Existing commitments continue; no new Meridian volume is approved.

## Expiry

Closes when the export review resolves or Corridor supply terms are finalized.

## Validation

- Darek does not approve foreign ownership unilaterally;
- effects remain recommendations until supported decision state exists;
- no guaranteed commodity price result;
- no-response preserves current state;
- option costs are visible;
- session scoped and idempotent.
