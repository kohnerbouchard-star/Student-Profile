## Purpose

Present sustainability, affordability, domestic reliability, export commitments, and project speed as competing constraints.

## Opening message

“Valerion can support the Corridor’s energy needs, but an export promise is only credible if domestic water and grid resilience remain protected. A green label is not a substitute for a measured condition.”

## Response options

### A. Binding environmental and water standards

- Benefit: strongest long-term resilience and credibility.
- Cost: higher construction cost and slower approval.
- Reply: Elia supports participation subject to measurable thresholds and review.

### B. Voluntary targets with public reporting

- Benefit: faster entry and lower administrative burden.
- Cost: weaker enforcement and greenwashing risk.
- Reply: Elia requests independent reporting and a trigger for mandatory action.

### C. Reduced energy export commitment

- Benefit: protects domestic flexibility.
- Cost: lower Corridor capacity and revenue.
- Reply: Elia proposes a smaller guaranteed baseline with optional surplus exports.

### D. Temporary imports and accelerated diversification

- Benefit: preserves near-term commitments while building resilience.
- Cost: higher cost, debt, and external dependence.
- Reply: Elia requests an affordability and financing plan.

### E. Continue full commitments without new conditions

- Benefit: fastest current execution.
- Cost: elevated drought, trust, and reliability risk.
- Reply: Elia records the risk and declines to certify resilience without additional evidence.

## No-response behavior

Existing commitments remain; no expanded Commission certification is issued.

## Expiry

Closes when energy terms are finalized or reservoir conditions materially change.

## Validation

- Commission certification distinct from national treaty approval;
- environmental options include costs;
- no guaranteed drought or recovery;
- household access visible;
- no-response neutral;
- response session scoped.
