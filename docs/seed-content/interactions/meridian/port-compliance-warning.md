## Purpose

Force a decision about risk controls, cost, route access, and insurance rather than treating compliance as either pure bureaucracy or automatic safety.

## Opening message

“Sableport can continue operating, but the margin for error is narrowing. If the Corridor expands volume or adds alternate routes, insurers need evidence that risk is being controlled rather than transferred invisibly.”

## Response options

### A. Apply full Yrethian standards to all routes

- Benefit: highest comparability and insurance confidence.
- Cost: higher compliance cost and slower Thaloris participation.
- Reply: Mira supports provisional coverage subject to verified implementation.

### B. Use tiered standards based on cargo risk

- Benefit: concentrates controls where losses would be greatest.
- Cost: classification complexity and risk of mislabeling.
- Reply: Mira requests independent cargo classification and audit rights.

### C. Allow emergency exceptions with time limits

- Benefit: preserves throughput during disruption.
- Cost: greater loss and precedent risk.
- Reply: Mira accepts a narrow emergency framework with expiry, exposure caps, and incident reporting.

### D. Rely on operator self-certification

- Benefit: lowest initial cost and fastest access.
- Cost: weak verification and likely higher premiums or reduced coverage.
- Reply: Mira records that coverage may be restricted; she does not claim an incident is certain.

## No-response behavior

Existing route classifications remain in force. New unreviewed routes receive no automatic Council endorsement.

## Expiry

Closes when route and coverage conditions are finalized or the capacity warning resolves.

## Validation

- Council does not control customs or ports;
- insurance outcome remains conditional;
- tiered and emergency options include administrative cost;
- self-certification is not automatically criminal;
- no-response does not create a hidden penalty;
- response stored once per session scope.
