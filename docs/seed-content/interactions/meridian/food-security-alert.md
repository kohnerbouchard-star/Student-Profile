## Purpose

Require a response to food-price pressure that considers producers, consumers, reserves, trade partners, currency, and time.

## Opening message

“The revised harvest estimate does not mean Eldoran is out of food. It means our buffer is smaller. Every response protects one group or period by placing cost somewhere else.”

## Response options

### A. Release strategic reserves

- Benefit: fastest price and supply relief.
- Cost: smaller future buffer and replenishment need.
- Reply: Halden supports a limited release with a replenishment trigger.

### B. Restrict selected exports

- Benefit: retains domestic supply.
- Cost: producer income, trade relationships, and foreign prices.
- Reply: Halden requests narrow scope and review rather than an open-ended ban.

### C. Arrange emergency imports

- Benefit: preserves exports and domestic supply.
- Cost: currency, shipping, and fiscal pressure.
- Reply: Halden requests route and financing confirmation.

### D. Provide targeted household support

- Benefit: protects vulnerable consumers while preserving price signals.
- Cost: fiscal burden and eligibility complexity.
- Reply: Halden supports targeted design and monitoring.

### E. Accept short-term prices and invest in capacity

- Benefit: avoids reserve depletion and supports long-term resilience.
- Cost: immediate household pressure and delayed benefit.
- Reply: Halden requests an interim protection plan for affected households.

## No-response behavior

Market and existing policy continue; no new intervention is assumed.

## Expiry

Closes when the harvest event resolves, escalates, or the policy window ends.

## Validation

- no option is costless;
- no-response does not secretly select austerity or intervention;
- producer and consumer effects visible;
- reserve behavior bounded;
- forecast uncertainty preserved;
- response session scoped.
