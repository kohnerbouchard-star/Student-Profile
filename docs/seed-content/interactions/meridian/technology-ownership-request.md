## Purpose

Present funding, licensing, interoperability, ownership, validation, and timing as separate choices.

## Opening message

“The Corridor can use Solvend’s optimization system, but deployment terms will determine where the long-term value remains. Funding, ownership, licensing, and interoperability are not the same decision.”

## Response options

### A. Exclusive investor ownership

- Benefit: fastest funding and centralized execution.
- Cost: reduced Solvend control and future licensing flexibility.
- Reply: Sena requests retained research rights and a defined technical-review process before accepting.

### B. Shared ownership among participating countries

- Benefit: broader alignment and access.
- Cost: slower governance and complex responsibility.
- Reply: Sena supports the concept if technical authority and maintenance obligations are explicit.

### C. Protected core patents with open interfaces

- Benefit: interoperability while preserving core value.
- Cost: licensing negotiation and interface-maintenance expense.
- Reply: Sena proposes a staged pilot and published interface standard.

### D. Delay deployment and retain full control

- Benefit: strongest independence and validation time.
- Cost: delayed Corridor efficiency and funding opportunity.
- Reply: Sena accepts the delay and warns that partners may adopt substitute systems.

## No-response behavior

No expanded deployment rights are granted. Existing research continues under current terms.

## Expiry

Closes when a technical architecture is selected or the pilot window ends.

## Validation

- interaction does not execute legal ownership transfer;
- options distinguish ownership, license, and interface;
- open interfaces are not costless;
- technical claims remain fictional and non-operational;
- response stored once per relevant decision window.
