## Purpose

Present Thaloris as a legitimate but higher-risk capacity option and require explicit choices about audit, insurance, speed, and long-term inclusion.

## Opening message

“Dusk Harbor can take selected Meridian cargo now. We have repair crews, bonded capacity, and alternate channels. The question is not whether we can move it. The question is which conditions your institutions will accept.”

## Response options

### A. Limited pilot with independent audit

- Benefit: tests capacity and records before broad commitment.
- Cost: slower relief and additional review expense.
- Reply: Tovan accepts the pilot and requests a fixed audit scope and decision date.

### B. Emergency routing with exposure limits

- Benefit: fastest relief for selected cargo.
- Cost: higher insurance cost and narrow time window.
- Reply: Tovan accepts volume and cargo caps but warns that excessive limits reduce practical value.

### C. Permanent secondary-route negotiation

- Benefit: long-term redundancy and inclusion.
- Cost: larger compliance and infrastructure commitment.
- Reply: Tovan requests recognition talks with Yrethia and the Forum.

### D. Decline and expand Sableport only

- Benefit: consistent regulated route.
- Cost: continued concentration and slower capacity relief.
- Reply: Tovan records the decision and redirects the Authority toward non-Corridor trade.

## No-response behavior

No Meridian cargo is rerouted. The offer expires without penalty.

## Expiry

Closes when the Sableport warning resolves or emergency routing is no longer useful.

## Validation

- no illegal-routing instruction;
- audit and emergency options have different timing and scope;
- permanent participation requires separate negotiation;
- no automatic Thaloris benefit from disruption;
- response and expiry are session scoped.
