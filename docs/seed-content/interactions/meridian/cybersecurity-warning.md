## Purpose

Create the central crisis decision around access, containment, privacy, national control, throughput, and uncertainty.

## Opening message

“We have confirmed that Meridian cargo and payment records no longer agree. We have not confirmed the cause. Every hour without shared visibility increases uncertainty, but access granted during an emergency can outlast the emergency unless its limits are explicit.”

## Response options

### A. Broad temporary access with expiry and audit

- Benefit: fastest shared visibility and coordinated containment.
- Cost: concentration, privacy, sovereignty, and oversight risk.
- Reply: Neris accepts only if scope, expiry, logging, and review authority are defined.

### B. Federated national response

- Benefit: preserves national control and limits central access.
- Cost: slower coordination, inconsistent data, and possible coverage gaps.
- Reply: Neris requests mandatory indicators and response deadlines from each national system.

### C. Technical assistance with independent oversight

- Benefit: creates a trust and review layer.
- Cost: added staffing, slower access approval, incomplete visibility, and coordination risk.
- Reply: Neris accepts but warns that oversight cannot replace operational access needed for containment.

### D. Manual verification and reduced throughput

- Benefit: limits reliance on compromised systems.
- Cost: congestion, labor demand, processing error, and fraud risk.
- Reply: Neris recommends prioritizing essential cargo and preserving evidence for later reconciliation.

### E. Limited Corridor suspension

- Benefit: protects integrity while the scope is investigated.
- Cost: trade delay, financial disruption, and confidence loss.
- Reply: Neris requests explicit restart conditions and preservation of records.

## No-response behavior

Emergency default must be defined by scenario or instructor policy. Recommended standard-profile default: limited manual verification for essential cargo only, with no permanent access grant.

No response must never imply broad surveillance consent.

## Expiry

Closes when the incident reaches containment decision, is cancelled by authorized action, or escalates to a separate event stage.

## Validation

- no tactical attack instructions;
- attribution remains unresolved;
- independent-oversight option has real delay and visibility costs;
- emergency access includes expiry;
- no-response policy visible before expiry;
- no credentials or private player data;
- response stored once per authorized decision owner and session.
