# Eco Novaria Country Lore v1

## Purpose

This document defines the ten Eco Novaria country identities used for simulation logic, market exposure, country events, stock-market shocks, sector behavior, company archetypes, map copy, future UI flavor text, and movement explanations.

Eco Novaria is a near-future geopolitical economy simulator. These countries are grounded geopolitical economies, not fantasy factions. Their lore should help the simulation explain why markets move, why sectors react differently, and how macro events flow through countries, companies, and students' classroom decisions.

## Design Rules

- Countries are grounded geopolitical economies, not fantasy factions.
- Every country should have economic strengths, vulnerabilities, market exposures, and event hooks.
- Country lore should support gameplay, stock-market movement explanations, and classroom economics.
- Lore should remain compatible with game-session-scoped simulations.
- Global country lore may be reused as template/reference data, but runtime market state must always be scoped to a `game_session_id`.
- The stock-market engine may derive country exposure templates from this document, but prices, events, ticks, regimes, holdings, orders, and fills must remain game-session runtime data.

## Northreach - Frostgate

### Concept

Arctic resource state and northern logistics power.

### Country Profile

Northreach is a cold, resource-rich country with long winters, difficult terrain, and high infrastructure costs. Its economy is built around rare minerals, natural gas, cold-climate engineering, defense infrastructure, and northern shipping routes. Frostgate is its main strategic city because it controls the safest passage through the northern corridor.

Northreach is disciplined, guarded, and infrastructure-heavy. It values self-sufficiency because its geography makes supply chains fragile. Other countries want access to its minerals and energy, but Northreach is cautious about foreign ownership and external control.

### Core Industries

- rare minerals
- natural gas
- energy security
- arctic logistics
- mining
- defense infrastructure
- cold-climate engineering
- northern shipping routes

### Market Exposure

Northreach stocks should react strongly to mineral demand, energy prices, defense spending, infrastructure investment, sanctions, arctic shipping access, and resource nationalism.

### Positive Market Events

- rare mineral demand rises
- energy prices increase
- northern shipping corridor opens
- defense infrastructure contracts expand
- mining productivity improves
- cold-weather engineering exports grow

### Negative Market Events

- mining accidents
- arctic port disruption
- foreign ownership dispute
- sanctions on resource exports
- infrastructure failure
- energy pipeline shutdown

### Stock Market Behavior

Northreach companies should be resource-sensitive, infrastructure-heavy, and moderately volatile. Energy and mining firms should move strongly on commodity shocks, sanctions, export restrictions, and defense demand. Logistics firms should respond to weather, port capacity, and northern route accessibility.

### Real-World Logic

- Arctic shipping
- resource nationalism
- energy security
- mining
- military positioning
- northern infrastructure

### Visual Identity

- frozen ports
- snow-covered industrial rail
- icebreakers
- mining facilities
- military radar stations
- aurora-lit logistics hubs

### One-Line Identity

Cold resource security and northern logistics.

## Yrethia - Sableport

### Concept

Maritime trade republic and shipping-finance hub.

### Country Profile

Yrethia is a coastal country whose power comes from its port system. Sableport is one of the busiest container ports in the world, handling imports, exports, ship repair, insurance, freight contracts, and customs processing. Yrethia's economy depends on keeping trade open, predictable, and legally enforceable.

It presents itself as rules-based and neutral, but it often benefits from instability elsewhere because disrupted trade gets rerouted through Sableport. It has strong banks, freight insurers, shipping companies, customs authorities, and port operators.

### Core Industries

- container shipping
- customs processing
- maritime insurance
- freight finance
- port automation
- ship repair
- transshipment
- logistics software

### Market Exposure

Yrethia stocks should react strongly to trade volume, shipping rates, port congestion, customs policy, maritime insurance costs, global demand, and regional instability.

### Positive Market Events

- global trade volume rises
- shipping lanes stabilize
- port throughput improves
- customs digitization increases efficiency
- insurance premiums rise without major losses
- trade reroutes through Sableport

### Negative Market Events

- port strike
- customs scandal
- shipping accident
- trade war
- insurance losses
- container backlog
- cyberattack on logistics systems

### Stock Market Behavior

Yrethian companies should be trade-cycle sensitive but comparatively stable when global rules are functioning. Port operators, insurers, banks, and shipping firms should rise when trade volume expands. They should fall on congestion, war-risk premiums, port shutdowns, and major insurance losses.

### Real-World Logic

- Singapore
- Rotterdam
- Busan
- maritime insurance
- customs hubs
- transshipment economies

### Visual Identity

- blue-lit harbors
- container ships
- automated cranes
- customs scanners
- rain
- naval patrol drones
- logistics dashboards

### One-Line Identity

Regulated maritime trade and port finance.

## Thaloris - Dusk Harbor

### Concept

High-risk port economy and informal trade gateway.

### Country Profile

Thaloris is a port nation that operates closer to the gray zone than Yrethia. Dusk Harbor is known for repair yards, bonded warehouses, secondary markets, salvage contracts, and informal trade networks. Thaloris is not necessarily criminal, but it is less regulated, more opportunistic, and more willing to operate where legal and commercial lines blur.

Thaloris becomes important whenever sanctions, shortages, or supply disruptions hit the world economy. Goods that cannot move directly often move indirectly through Thaloris. This gives the country influence, but also a reputation problem.

### Core Industries

- re-export trade
- salvage
- ship repair
- bonded warehouses
- secondary markets
- informal logistics
- gray-zone finance
- repair yards

### Market Exposure

Thaloris stocks should react strongly to sanctions, supply shortages, logistics disruption, salvage demand, trade restrictions, and regulatory crackdowns.

### Positive Market Events

- sanctions increase indirect trade demand
- shipping disruptions create repair/salvage demand
- shortages raise secondary-market margins
- Dusk Harbor expands bonded warehouse capacity
- high-risk logistics contracts increase

### Negative Market Events

- regulatory investigation
- customs seizure
- sanctions enforcement
- port blacklisting
- reputational crisis
- insurance withdrawal
- informal finance crackdown

### Stock Market Behavior

Thaloris companies should be opportunistic and volatile. They can benefit from disruption but are exposed to reputation, enforcement, and insurance risk. Their markets should rise during shortages and rerouting events, but fall sharply during crackdowns.

### Real-World Logic

- free ports
- gray-market trade
- sanctions evasion risk
- re-export hubs

- salvage
- informal logistics

### Visual Identity

### One-Line Identity

High-risk shipping, re-export trade, and salvage markets.

## Eldoran - Crescent Bay

### Concept

Central market economy and agricultural-logistics anchor.

Eldoran sits near the center of the map and functions as the stabilizing trade hub. It has fertile land, strong internal transport, a major bay, and the most important wholesale markets. Crescent Bay is where food, consumer goods, commodities, and financial contracts are priced.

Eldoran's strength is balance. It is not the most militarized, richest, or most technologically advanced country, but everyone depends on it. If Eldoran's currency, food supply, or transport system breaks down, prices rise everywhere.

# Economic Blocs

## Northern Resource Bloc

Countries: Northreach and Solvend.

Northreach has minerals, energy, and northern logistics. Solvend has the research capacity, technical expertise, and aerospace/AI industries to make those resources strategically valuable. Their relationship is practical but tense: Northreach controls hard assets, while Solvend controls high-value knowledge.

Market logic: Resource shocks in Northreach can affect Solvend technology and aerospace firms. Solvend breakthroughs can increase demand for Northreach minerals.

## Western Maritime Corridor

Countries: Yrethia and Thaloris.

Yrethia is the clean, regulated port economy. Thaloris is the flexible, high-risk logistics economy. They compete, but they also need each other. Yrethia handles predictable legal trade. Thaloris absorbs irregular, disrupted, or indirect flows.

Market logic: Trade disruption can hurt Yrethia's stability but benefit Thaloris's opportunistic logistics. Regulatory crackdowns can benefit Yrethia and hurt Thaloris.

## Central Stability Zone

Countries: Eldoran, Valerion, and Lumenor.

These countries keep the world functioning through food, services, finance, education, diplomacy, infrastructure, and civic legitimacy. Eldoran stabilizes food and commodities. Valerion stabilizes clean energy, water, premium services, and finance. Lumenor stabilizes institutions, education, diplomacy, and legitimacy.

Market logic: When the Central Stability Zone is strong, global volatility should decrease. If Eldoran food, Valerion energy/water, or Lumenor diplomatic legitimacy breaks down, global prices should become more unstable.

## Eastern Pressure Zone

Countries: Xalvoria, Dravenlok, and Syndalis.

This is where capital, industry, and information power collide. Xalvoria controls money and infrastructure finance. Dravenlok controls heavy production and defense manufacturing. Syndalis controls data, cybersecurity, and covert market influence.

Market logic: The Eastern Pressure Zone should produce larger geopolitical market shocks. Xalvoria debt diplomacy, Dravenlok industrial nationalism, and Syndalis cyber influence can each move global sectors in different ways.
